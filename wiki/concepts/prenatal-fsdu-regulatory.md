# Prenatal FSDU Regulatory Framework

The [[domains/prenatal/_index|prenatal brand]] must register under FSSAI's **FSDU (Food for Special Dietary Use)** category — NOT as a general "Health Supplement." This is a critical strategic decision because the Health Supplement category would require a warning saying "not for pregnant women," which kills the brand.

## FSDU category — why it's mandatory

- FSDU explicitly recognizes pregnant/lactating women as a target population
- Health Supplement category = mandatory "not for pregnant women" warning = brand death
- FSDU allows structure/function claims with scientific backing

## Label requirements (FSDU)

All labels must include:
1. "FOOD FOR SPECIAL DIETARY USE — Food for Pregnant and Lactating Women"
2. "NOT FOR MEDICINAL USE" (prominently)
3. "RECOMMENDED TO BE USED UNDER MEDICAL ADVICE ONLY"
4. Target consumer group + rationale for use + properties description
5. Dosage instructions, source of bioactive ingredients, storage warnings

## What you CAN claim

- Structure/function claims with scientific backing
- "Specially formulated for pregnant women"
- "Supports maternal nutrition"
- "Provides essential nutrients for fetal development"

## What you CANNOT claim

- NO therapeutic claims: "prevents birth defects," "treats anemia," "cures morning sickness"
- NO "pregnancy safe" on label — implies medical safety guarantee
- Use "specially formulated for pregnant women" instead

## FSSAI vs CDSCO line (critical)

- All nutrients must stay within **ICMR Recommended Dietary Allowance (1x RDA)**
- Exceeding limits (e.g., Vitamin D3 > 1000 IU) triggers **CDSCO drug classification** = clinical trials required
- Stay firmly in FSSAI territory

## Advertising rules

- FSSAI Advertising and Claims Regulations (2018) apply to ALL marketing
- Instagram, website, influencer content must all comply
- Health benefit claims must be backed by peer-reviewed published evidence
