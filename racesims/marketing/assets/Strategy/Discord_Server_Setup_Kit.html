Day 2 — First Race Engineer Tip

**#race-engineer-tips:** **Race Engineer Tip #1: Trail Braking — The Technique That Separates Fast Drivers** Most sim racers brake in a straight line and then turn. Fast drivers overlap braking and turning — this is called trail braking. Here's the concept: As you approach a corner, begin braking at your normal point. But instead of releasing the brake completely before turn-in, gradually ease off the brake AS you turn the steering wheel. Why it works: - Keeps weight on the front tires during turn-in = more front grip - Lets you brake later without going wide - Gives you more control over rotation The key word is "gradually." You're trading braking force for steering force smoothly. Try it in ACC at Monza T1. Brake at the 100m board, but keep 10-15% brake pressure as you turn in. You'll feel the car rotate more willingly. This is exactly what I teach real racing drivers. The sim is the perfect place to practice it. Any questions? Drop them below.
