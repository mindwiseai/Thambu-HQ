# CDRI-55 — Extract Brand Name

Proposed trademarked brand name for the [[cdri-08|CDRI-08]] Bacopa extract, decided during the [[2026-onedrive-mindwise-folder|April 8 packaging call]] between [[thambu]], [[ankita]], and [[shivendra-selvam]].

## Why a new name is needed

[[thambu]] explained the strategic reasoning:

1. **CDRI-08 is not owned by Mindwise.** KeenMind Australia uses it. Other brands reference it. Mindwise needs its own trademarked extract name.
2. **Future market expansion:** When entering new markets (like the Philippines model), distributors own the product brand name, but the **extract name** needs to be owned by [[lumen-marketing-company|LMC]]. Example: "Brain Booster" (distributor brand) would "contain CDRI-55" (LMC's extract).
3. **Competitors like Synapsa and KSM-66** have branded their extracts. Mindwise needs the same.

## How CDRI-55 was chosen

From the April 8 call:

- [[shivendra-selvam]] proposed "CDRI-55" — combining CDRI (recognizable from research papers) with 55 (the optimal bacoside percentage)
- [[ankita]] endorsed it: "It literally has to be a few words and a number... CDRI-55, we can recognize this"
- [[thambu]]: "If you say CDRI-55, you can always relate it to CDRI-08 because all research mentions CDRI-08"
- The "55" also serves as the **only place** they hint at the 55% bacoside number, since [[thambu]] insists on NOT mentioning "55%" explicitly on packaging (competitors would copy)

## Trademark status

- **Bhaskar** (trademark expert, friend of [[thambu]]) is "already on the job" checking trademark availability for CDRI-55
- Previous trademark: "Mindwise CDRI" exists but is "too big a name for the extract"
- [[mindcaps-proposal|MindCaps]] was previously proposed by [[shivendra-selvam]] but dismissed as not holding enough weight

## Where CDRI-55 will appear

- **Bottle side 3:** "Clinically proven CDRI-55" badge with CSIR-CDRI logo above and QR code below
- **All marketing materials, website, and copy** — as the branded ingredient name
- **Future distributor agreements** — distributors brand the product, but all contain "CDRI-55"

## Why NOT just use 55% explicitly

[[thambu]] gave critical context in the April 8 call:

- CDRI researched 75%, 80%, 100% bacoside concentrations — **100% is toxic, 80% didn't work, 75% didn't work**
- 55% (plus/minus 5%) is the **only concentration that worked** in clinical trials
- The remaining 45% contains **other therapeutically relevant constituents** (bacosides A and B plus additional compounds)
- CDRI themselves advised: **"Don't push that 55% because everyone will start using your materials"**
- Historical precedent: KeenMind Australia's "Volga" highlighted "Brahmi" generically — cheap Brahmi flooded Australia and killed her market
- Instead, use the word **"optimum"** — as recommended by CDRI scientists
