# Pricing Strategy: ₹850–900 MRP

Locked in [[2026-03-24-meeting-manik-pricing-website|the March 24 meeting with Manik]]. **Target MRP: ₹850–900 per bottle** for the [[60-capsule-bottle-decision|60-capsule D2C SKU]].

## Manik's reasoning

| Price band | Verdict | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **₹300–400** (Himalaya, generic Bacopa) | ❌ Too mass-market | Doesn't match the [[your-unfair-advantage|premium brand positioning]]. Signals low efficacy. |
| **₹600 and below** | ❌ Doesn't match positioning | Same problem. Low price = "Placebo Wellness" perception (see [[placebo-wellness-enemy]]) |
| **₹850–900** | ✅ **SWEET SPOT** | Premium enough to command respect; accessible enough to not require outsized creator-marketing budgets |
| **Above ₹1,000** | ❌ Requires premium creator partnerships (₹2-3L per creator/month). Wellbeing Nutrition / Ace tier. Not viable at current marketing budget. | |
| **₹4,000-5,000** | ❌ Too premium for the launch market. Top of category. | |

## Earlier pricing reference (now superseded)

The original target in [[2026-03-17-meeting-binita-strategic-decisions|the March 17 Binita meeting]] was ₹1,200 — **middle positioning**. This was *deferred* pending Sorted's recommendation, which Manik delivered on March 24 as ₹850–900. Anywhere in the wiki or older docs that mentions ₹1,200 should be considered stale.

## Discount discipline (HARD RULE)

Per Manik (and consistent with prior discussions):

- **Never discount on single-bottle MRP.** Protects the anchor price.
- **Bundle discounts only.** E.g., buy 2 get 10% off, buy 3 get 15% off.
- **No deep discounting (50-60%) ever.** Customers buying at deep discounts rarely repurchase at full price. Damages premium brand perception.
- **Heavy discounting** is ONLY for stock clearance or trial push — never an ongoing strategy.

This rule is non-negotiable and should be flagged anytime discount strategy comes up.

## Premium pricing = premium content spend

Manik's downstream insight:
> "Premium pricing = premium content spend. Every pricing decision has downstream implications on the kind of creators, content quality, and marketing budget required."

In other words: pricing at ₹850–900 commits Mindwise to a content quality bar. You can't price premium and ship cheap content.

## Bundle structure (proposed in [[launch-plan-june-1-2026]] Phase 2)
- Buy 2 → 10% off
- Buy 3 → 15% off

This is also operationally important because:
- A 60-cap bottle = 1 month adult supply (2 caps/day)
- [[8-12-week-onset-claim|Peak results require 8-12 weeks]] = need 2-3 bottles to feel effect
- Bundle pricing = first-purchase commitment that matches the science

## Marketing budget context

The ₹850–900 price has to support:
- Total Year 1 marketing budget: ₹25 lakhs (₹10L content + ₹10L performance + ₹5L influencer)
- Sales target: 10,000 bottles/month by end of Year 1
- = ₹85L–90L monthly revenue at target = annual ~₹10 cr

The math only closes if:
1. CAC stays below ~₹400 / acquired customer
2. Repurchase rate hits a healthy multiple (community vision pays off)
3. Bundle attach rate is high (offsetting the headline price)
