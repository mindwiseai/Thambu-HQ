## Positioning

Each product listing on racesims.in answers: *"Why would a race engineer bother making this?"* — per [[race-engineer-positioning]]. Separates RaceSims from generic Thingiverse-STL resellers.

- **Measured, not guessed.** Parts measured against real hardware (e.g., Conspit 310 Apex), tolerances published.
- **Functional, not decorative.** Phone holder works for telemetry apps; cup holder holds an isotonic sports bottle.
- **Durability documented.** 100-hour vibration test + 48-hour 45°C heat soak before any SKU is listed.
- **Subtle branding.** RaceSims red is one accent; matte black ASA is the body.
- **Installed in < 60 seconds** with QR-linked install video.

## Content plan per SKU

1. Product page on racesims.in with FAQ schema (GEO-compliant per [[geo-content-readiness]]).
2. Instagram Reel of the part in use on a rig.
3. Process Reel (print → install → use).
4. Technical post (printer settings, material choice, tolerance math) — the race-engineer content moat.

## Phase-2 candidates (for after week 8)

Already sketched, not yet prioritised:
- Cross-brand adapter plates (Moza ↔ Conspit, Simagic ↔ VNM) — [[moza]] and [[vnm]] ecosystem bridges.
- Replacement cup holders / knobs for competitor rigs — cross-brand service revenue.
- Button box housings (modular, customer-configured).
- Monitor bezels / display brackets for triple-screen setups.
- GoPro / webcam / mic arm mounts for content-creator customers.
- Kiosk sensor covers + emergency-stop button housings for B2B (e.g. [[online-instruments]], [[araya-airport]]).

Full model in [[2026-04-23-3d-printing-capex-decision]]. Headline: steady-state ~₹53k net/mo, Year 1 net ~₹4.96L, capex payback 3–6 months, ~320% Year 1 ROI.

## Hard constraints

- Brand name must always appear as "RaceSims" (one word) per [[brand-name-consistency-issue]].
- No SKU whose listing could be written by a non-race-engineer. If the copy is generic, either reject the SKU or rewrite the copy.
- Do not use [[virtual-racing-hub|VRH]] (or any competitor) by name in listings — compete on content depth, not by attacking.
- Never ship PLA parts to customers (deforms at Indian summer temperatures).
