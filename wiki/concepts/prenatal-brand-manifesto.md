# The Edit — Brand Manifesto

**Locked 2026-04-14.** The brand's reason for existing.

## The manifesto

**Core idea:** *The wellness brand India was missing.*

**Full paragraph:**

> The Edit exists because something specific has been missing for Indian couples and parents-to-be: a modern, transparent, gynaecologist-co-formulated brand with every ingredient traceable to a named source, every dose backed by published science, and every formula designed for a specific stage of the family-building journey. Not imported. Not pharma-coded. Not opaque. Built in India, across preconception, pregnancy, and the months after.

## The defensible positioning

The specific gap in the Indian market is that **no single brand combines all of**:

1. DTC subscription model (not doctor/pharmacy distribution)
2. Modern lifestyle branding (not pharma-coded)
3. Transparent, traceable branded ingredients (Quatrefolic, life'sDHA, Albion, KSM-66)
4. Gynaecologist co-formulation (not just endorsement)
5. Designed for the full family-building journey — preconception, prenatal, postnatal
6. Published-science-backed claims for every promise
7. Made in India, priced to be accessible

Individual Indian brands have some of these:
- [[trimacare|Trimacare]] — has ingredient quality + medical rigor, but is pharma-coded + doctor-channel
- Wellbeing Nutrition — has DTC + lifestyle, but prenatal isn't their hero
- Inatal / Pregomega Plus / Autrin — Indian-made prenatals, but prescription-style, no brand story
- Himalaya / Patanjali — Indian-made, Ayurvedic framework (different tradition)
- OZiva, Kapiva, Fast&Up — Indian DTC wellness but not prenatal-focused

**Nobody combines all seven.** That's the white space The Edit occupies.

## Supporting brand pillars

These flow from the manifesto but are not the manifesto itself:

- **Essentialism** — nothing in the bottle that hasn't earned its place
- **Couples-inclusive (at preconception stage)** — couples bundle for Him + Her preconception products
- **Lifecycle brand** — walks with customers from preconception through postnatal
- **Founder-led credibility** — [[aishwarya-chandrasekaran|Aishu]] as founder, gynaecologist as co-founder

## Short-form derivatives

From the manifesto, the taglines and campaign lines derive naturally:

- **"The wellness brand India was missing."** (primary headline)
- **"Built in India, for every stage."**
- **"Every ingredient traceable. Every formula doctor-made."**
- **"Nothing imported. Nothing opaque. Nothing that hasn't earned its place."**

## What this manifesto replaces

Earlier versions considered and superseded:

- **"TTC is a team job. Men need to be equally invested."** ([[prenatal-brand-manifesto-couples-equality]]) — still TRUE as a supporting value, but too narrow as a brand manifesto because it only applies to preconception. The Edit needs a manifesto that works for Prenatal and Postnatal equally.
- **"Together from the Start."** — considered as a warmer India-apt framing, but felt too generic and lacked the specific problem/solution narrative.
- **"Reproductive Partnership."** — considered but read as academic for the Indian mass market.

The locked manifesto (above) captures why the brand exists in one paragraph, works for all SKUs, and is defensible against factual challenge.
