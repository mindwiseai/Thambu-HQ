# Ritual

US-based prenatal supplement brand. The primary benchmark for [[aishwarya-chandrasekaran|Aishu's]] [[domains/prenatal/_index|prenatal brand]].

## Key facts

- Founded 2016 by Katerina Schneider (VC investor, pregnant, couldn't find a trusted prenatal)
- $250M+ gross revenue by 2024, ~70% gross margins
- DTC subscription at ~$30/month for first 6 years before retail (Whole Foods 2022, Target 2023)
- "Made Traceable" program — full ingredient supply chain transparency
- 24-week randomized double-blind clinical study (Brooklyn College/CUNY, Maimonides Medical Center, Cornell)
- Delayed-release capsule design, Clean Label Project Purity Award, third-party heavy metal testing

## What to replicate for India

- Transparency-first positioning (ingredient sourcing visibility)
- DTC subscription model before retail expansion
- Clinical study as marketing asset (not just regulatory requirement)
- Lifestyle branding over pharma-coded aesthetics
- Single comprehensive prenatal formula (not trimester-specific like [[trimacare]])

## What differs for India

- FSSAI FSDU regulatory path (not FDA) — see [[prenatal-fsdu-regulatory]]
- Must stay within ICMR RDA (1x limit, not US RDA)
- Price sensitivity: Rs.800-1,500 vs $30/month
- Doctor deference culture requires gynecologist co-founder, not just brand credibility
