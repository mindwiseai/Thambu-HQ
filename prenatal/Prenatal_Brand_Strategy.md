# Prenatal & Postnatal Supplement Brand — India

## Project Overview

Building India's first transparency-led, gynecologist-formulated, direct-to-consumer (DTC) prenatal and postnatal supplement brand, modeled on Ritual (US brand, $250M+ revenue). The founder (Thambu) personally experienced the trust gap — relying on imported supplements during pregnancy because Indian options felt untrustworthy.

## Core Brand Vision

- **Clean, traceable ingredients** — every ingredient sourced from named suppliers, fully transparent
- **Gynecologist co-formulated** — not just endorsed, but co-founded by a practicing OB-GYN
- **DTC subscription model** — direct website sales, not doctor/pharmacy distribution
- **Lifestyle branding** — premium wellness brand, not pharma-coded packaging
- **Backed by science** — published literature at launch, own clinical study by Year 2-3

## Key Research Completed

- Founded 2016 by Katerina Schneider (VC investor, pregnant, couldn't find a trusted prenatal)
- $250M+ gross revenue by 2024, ~70% gross margins
- DTC subscription at ~$30/month for first 6 years before entering retail (Whole Foods 2022, Target 2023)
- "Made Traceable" program — full ingredient supply chain transparency

- Delayed-release capsule design, Clean Label Project Purity Award, third-party heavy metal testing

- **Trimacare** (main competitor): Founded by Sameer Agarwal & Surbhi Gupta (PlusPlus Lifesciences), launched Jan 2021
  - Patented trimester-specific Multi-Micronutrient (MMN) model (Trimacare 1, 2, 3)
  - Uses L-Methylfolate, time-release iron, vegetarian Omega-3
  - 5,000+ doctors prescribing, 100,000+ patients
  - Pharma-coded branding, doctor-driven distribution — NOT DTC lifestyle brand
  - Their patent on trimester-specific MMN must be navigated (differentiate formulation)
- No Indian brand is doing Ritual-style transparency + DTC + lifestyle branding for prenatal

## Regulatory Framework (CRITICAL)

### Product Category: FSDU (Food for Special Dietary Use)
- Must register under FSSAI's **FSDU category** — NOT as a general "Health Supplement"
- FSDU explicitly recognizes pregnant/lactating women as a target population
- Health Supplement category would REQUIRE a warning saying "not for pregnant women" — kills the brand

### Label Requirements (FSDU)
- Must include: "FOOD FOR SPECIAL DIETARY USE — Food for Pregnant and Lactating Women"
- Must include: "NOT FOR MEDICINAL USE" (prominently)
- Must include: "RECOMMENDED TO BE USED UNDER MEDICAL ADVICE ONLY"
- Must include: target consumer group, rationale for use, properties description
- Must include: dosage instructions, source of bioactive ingredients, storage warnings

### What You CAN Claim
- Structure/function claims with scientific backing: "specially formulated for pregnant women," "supports maternal nutrition," "provides essential nutrients for fetal development"

### What You CANNOT Claim
- NO therapeutic claims: cannot say "prevents birth defects," "treats anemia," "cures morning sickness"
- NO "pregnancy safe" on label — implies medical safety guarantee, could be interpreted as therapeutic
- Use "specially formulated for pregnant women" instead

### FSSAI vs. CDSCO Line
- All nutrients must stay within ICMR Recommended Dietary Allowance (RDA) — cannot exceed 1x RDA
- Exceeding limits (e.g., Vitamin D3 > 1000 IU) triggers CDSCO drug classification = clinical trials required
- Stay firmly in FSSAI territory

### Advertising Rules
- FSSAI Advertising and Claims Regulations (2018) apply to ALL marketing, not just labels
- Instagram, website, influencer content must all comply
- Health benefit claims must be backed by peer-reviewed published evidence

## Proving Efficacy (Phased Approach)

1. **Phase 1 — Launch with published science (Day 1):** Published peer-reviewed literature on each ingredient (L-Methylfolate, DHA, chelated iron, etc.) — sufficient for FSSAI compliance
2. **Phase 2 — Observational study (6-12 months):** Partner with hospital gynecologists to track outcomes in women already taking the product — lower ethics burden than interventional trial
3. **Phase 3 — Comparative clinical study (Year 2-3):** Head-to-head study (your product vs. standard Indian prenatal), measuring blood biomarkers — Ritual's playbook. Partner with AIIMS or top medical college. Becomes biggest marketing asset
4. **Additional trust signals:** Clean Label Project certification, third-party heavy metal testing every batch, USP/NSF verification

### Note on Pregnant Women in Research
- Classified as vulnerable population under ICMR National Ethical Guidelines
- Full ethics committee review required (not expedited)
- Supplement comparison studies (not drug trials) with minimal fetal risk can qualify for approval

## Gynecologist Co-Founder Strategy

- **Must be co-founder level** — not advisor, not consultant
- Drives formulation decisions, provides clinical credibility, regulatory strength, marketing asset
- Ideally: practicing OB-GYN with public presence (Instagram/YouTube) educating women on pregnancy health
- Position brand as "science-led company with medical professionals" — NOT "Dr. So-and-so's vitamin"
- The gynecologist adds credibility to the brand; the brand doesn't become the gynecologist

## Key Risks

- **Pricing sensitivity:** ₹800-1,500/month is premium for India; target urban metros initially
- **Trimacare's patent:** Avoid trimester-specific MMN model; single comprehensive prenatal (like Ritual) works
- **FSSAI/CDSCO line:** Keep all nutrients within ICMR RDA; hire regulatory consultant
- **Consumer education:** Indian women defer to doctors; need content strategy that educates before selling
- **Gynecologist search:** Don't settle for advisor role; hold out for co-founder commitment

## Strategic Decision: Lifestyle vs. Pharma-Coded

Decided on **lifestyle brand with clinical backing** (hybrid approach):
- Lead with DTC lifestyle brand as primary identity and revenue engine
- Use gynecologist co-founder for clinical credibility feeding into the lifestyle brand
- Can add doctor outreach and pharmacy distribution later as growth channel
- Starting pharma-coded makes lifestyle pivot very hard later; starting lifestyle allows adding pharma channels
