## The Core Idea

The Edit exists because something specific has been missing for Indian couples and parents-to-be: a modern, transparent, gynaecologist-co-formulated brand with every ingredient traceable to a named source, every dose backed by published science, and every formula designed for a specific stage of the family-building journey. Not imported. Not pharma-coded. Not opaque. Built in India, across preconception, pregnancy, and the months after.

---

## The Gap in the Market

Indian-made prenatal and family-planning supplements do exist today. What *doesn't* exist is an Indian brand that combines all of the following:

- DTC subscription model (not doctor-prescribed, not pharmacy-distributed)
- Modern lifestyle branding (not pharma-coded packaging or clinical design)
- Transparent, traceable branded ingredients (e.g., Quatrefolic methylfolate, life'sDHA algal DHA, Albion chelated minerals)
- Gynaecologist co-formulation (not just doctor endorsement)
- Designed for the full family-building journey (preconception, prenatal, postnatal — in one brand)
- Every claim backed by published clinical evidence
- Made in India, priced to be accessible (~Rs.800–1,500 per SKU per month)

Existing brands have parts of this. Trimacare is clinically robust but pharma-coded and doctor-channel. Wellbeing Nutrition is DTC and lifestyle-branded but prenatal is not their hero. Ayurvedic brands (Himalaya, Patanjali) operate on a different traditional framework. Imported brands (Ritual, Garden of Life) are expensive and hard to access. Generic multivitamins don't address motherhood's specific needs.

No single Indian brand combines all seven. That is the white space The Edit occupies.

## Product Architecture

### Day 1 Launch — 3 SKUs

**1. Edit For Two (the hero SKU)**
Sold as one box containing two bottles. One product — for two people.

- **For Her:** egg quality, fertility support, methylfolate, CoQ10, myo-inositol, DHA. FSDU regulatory pathway.
- **For Him:** sperm quality, motility, antioxidants, CoQ10, L-carnitine, zinc, selenium, ashwagandha (KSM-66). Health Supplement regulatory pathway.

**2. Edit Prenatal**
Comprehensive 18–25 ingredient prenatal supplement with branded traceable ingredients (Quatrefolic, life'sDHA, Albion chelated minerals). All within ICMR RDA. FSDU regulatory pathway.

### Year 1 Expansion

**3. Edit Postnatal (Month 9–12 post-launch)**
Postpartum and lactation support, formulated for the mother's recovery and the breastfeeding period.

## Why Launch Edit For Two + Prenatal at Day 1 (NOT All Three Together)

Three options were considered:

### Option A — Launch all 3 SKUs simultaneously

**Rejected.** Postnatal has no Day 1 customer demand. The natural Postnatal customer is your own Prenatal customer who just gave birth — and that cohort doesn't exist until Month 9–12 after launch. Manufacturing Postnatal at Day 1 means inventory sits in the warehouse for nearly a year before customers materialize, locking up capital and burning shelf life.

### Option B — Launch Prenatal only

**Rejected.** Misses the couples-inclusive positioning entirely. The brand's manifesto — that the family-building journey needs a brand built for all of it — is undercut on Day 1. Prenatal-only makes The Edit a maternity brand, not the full-journey brand it is meant to be.

### Option C — Launch Edit For Two + Prenatal at Day 1, add Postnatal at Month 9–12

**Selected.** Four reasons:

1. **The brand's manifesto is visible from Day 1.** Edit For Two addresses preconception for both partners. Edit Prenatal continues the journey. The couples-inclusive full-journey positioning is lived at launch, not deferred.
2. **Full customer journey captured.** A woman who buys Edit For Two while planning her pregnancy transitions naturally to Edit Prenatal when she conceives — same brand, same trust, same subscription.
3. **Postnatal launches at peak demand.** First Prenatal cohort gives birth at Month 9–12. Postnatal ships exactly when this cohort needs it.
4. **Two PR moments instead of one.** Day 1 launch is the brand's opening statement. Month 9–12 Postnatal launch is a second wave of media attention.

## Regulatory + Operational Foundation

**Operating entity:** **The Edit is being incorporated as its own independent Private Limited company** — separate from any of the founder's other ventures. This protects both The Edit and the founder's existing businesses from cross-liability. Clean cap table for future equity allocation (including the gynaecologist co-founder) and any future fundraise.

**Regulatory pathways (fresh applications for the new entity):**
- **FSSAI Central License** — new application under The Edit Pvt Ltd
- **FSDU (Foods for Special Dietary Use)** endorsement — for Preconception Her + Prenatal + Postnatal
- **Health Supplement** endorsement — for Preconception Him

**Manufacturing:** India domestic only. Currently evaluating Tanishq Lifecare (Ahmedabad) and Brukem Life Care (Ahmedabad) as contract manufacturers.

**Formulation philosophy:** All nutrients within ICMR RDA. Branded traceable ingredients (Quatrefolic, life'sDHA, Albion, KSM-66). No fillers. Nothing without published evidence. Premium ingredients, essentialist formula.

## Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Indian men don't proactively buy supplements | Couples bundle ensures the woman buys for both; packaging + copy make his daily ritual frictionless |
| Cultural conservatism around openly discussing family planning | Target urban metro educated 28–38 demographic first; warm family-journey framing avoids confrontational positioning |
| Trimacare patent on trimester-specific MMN | Single comprehensive prenatal (like Ritual) sidesteps the patent entirely |
| Manufacturer FSDU license gap | Verification questions sent to Tanishq + Brukem; if neither qualifies, pivot to shortlist alternatives |
| New entity means slower regulatory start (vs using an existing FSSAI license) | Adds 2–4 months to timeline but is the right long-term call — clean liability boundary for both The Edit and the founder's other ventures |
| Gynaecologist co-founder search | Step 0 of execution — the brand cannot launch without one. Clean Pvt Ltd structure makes equity allocation straightforward |
