# Calabrese et al. 2008 — Elderly Standardized Bacopa Trial

Clinical trial in the [[cdri-08|CDRI-08]] / Mindwise evidence base targeting **non-depressed elderly**, with the unusual feature of measuring multiple psychological dimensions (cognition, anxiety, depression) simultaneously.

## Citation

> Calabrese et al. (2008). Effects of a Standardized *Bacopa monnieri* Extract on Cognitive Performance, Anxiety, and Depression in the Elderly: A Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Trial. *J. Alternative & Complementary Medicine*, 14(6):707-713.

## Study design

- **Format:** Double-blind, placebo-controlled, randomized
- **Cohort:** Non-depressed elderly
- **Methodology:** Battery of cognitive tests + anxiety + depression measures
- **Sample size:** Low (per CTD assessment)

## Findings

- ✅ **Positive benefits on multiple measures of cognitive performance**
- ✅ Effects observed across cognition, anxiety, and depression dimensions

## Quality rating
**Medium** — per CTD assessment:
- Demonstrated positive benefits on multiple cognitive measures
- "Rigorous but deals with multiple variables and multiple parameters" (multiple-comparison concern)
- Low sample size
- Subject restriction: non-depressed elderly only (limits generalizability)

## Significance for Mindwise

This trial sits in the broader **elderly cohort evidence base** alongside [[barbhaiya-2008-elderly-trial]] and [[morgan-stevens-2010-elderly]]. Together they provide:
- Multiple independent confirmations of cognitive benefit in elderly populations
- Evidence that extends beyond memory alone (anxiety + depression dimensions also touched)
- Direct relevance to the [[four-cohorts|aging cohort]] in Mindwise's audience map

## What it supports
✅ "Improves cognitive performance in elderly populations"
✅ Backing for the [[four-cohorts|aging cohort]] positioning
✅ "Multiple measures of cognition" — supports broader rather than narrow claims
✅ Some support for anxiety / mood benefits in elderly

## What it does NOT support
❌ Treatment claims for depression — the cohort was non-depressed
❌ Single-trial claims — must be cited alongside other elderly trials for credibility
❌ Generalization to younger cohorts
