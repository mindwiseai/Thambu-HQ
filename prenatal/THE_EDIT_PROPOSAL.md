## The Core Idea

**The Edit is built on one belief: making a baby is a reproductive partnership, not a woman's solo project.**

In India, when couples decide to have a baby, only one person is asked to prepare. She visits doctors, takes vitamins, changes her diet, monitors her cycle. He continues life unchanged. But conception is a team sport — sperm quality matters as much as egg quality. Stress affects both. Lifestyle affects both. Recovery affects both.

The Edit is a wellness brand for couples who believe the work of building a family begins with two people, not one.

---

## Why "Reproductive Partnership" Is the Right Framing

We chose this term deliberately. Other vocabulary doesn't do the work:

| Term | What it implies | Why we don't lead with it |
|---|---|---|
| "Fertility" | Medicalized — implies infertility, clinics, treatment | Stigmatizing in Indian context; positions brand as a problem-fixer |
| "TTC" (Trying to Conceive) | Insider lingo — clinical, reductive | Doesn't articulate the *why* behind the work |
| "Couples Planning" | Logistical — feels like calendar management | Misses the physiological reality |
| **"Reproductive Partnership"** | A relationship, a shared physiological project, a values stance | **Captures the truth + signals the brand's worldview** |

**Reproductive partnership** is a category-defining phrase because:

1. **It names what's missing in India's wellness landscape.** No brand articulates that conception is a partnership. Saying it out loud is itself a contribution.
2. **It works as a value, a category, and a marketing platform simultaneously.** It tells customers what we believe, what we sell, and how we talk about reproduction.
3. **It's defensible.** Trimacare cannot adopt this language credibly — their entire model is doctor-prescribed-to-woman. Ritual (US) sells two products but doesn't lead with partnership. Ayurvedic brands lean on dosha balance, not equality.
4. **It opens content territory others can't enter.** Male fertility, paternal preparation, the unspoken work of motherhood, partner accountability — all become The Edit's natural conversation, and competitors look out of step the moment they try to participate.

## The Market Insight

**India is the largest reproductive health market in the world that nobody is building intentionally for.**

- 27 million pregnancies per year
- ~5-8 million couples actively trying to conceive at any given time
- India health & wellness market: $164B (2025) → $258B (2034)
- Asia-Pacific holds 38.58% of global postpartum market

But the existing brands fail in specific ways:

- **Trimacare** (and similar pharma-coded prenatals) — woman-only, doctor-prescribed, treats the woman as a patient
- **Imported brands** (Ritual, Garden of Life) — expensive, hard to access, regulatory grey zone, no India context
- **Ayurvedic brands** (Himalaya, Patanjali) — pharma-coded, no traceability, no transparency
- **Nobody at all** — addresses the male side of conception, or the couple as the unit of care

**The white space:** A premium DTC wellness brand built on reproductive partnership, with traceable ingredients, gynecologist co-formulation, and transparent sourcing. Modeled on Ritual but for India, and broader than Ritual (couples, not just women).

## Product Architecture

### Day 1 launch (3 SKUs)

**1. Edit Couples Bundle (the hero SKU)**
Sold as one box containing two bottles. Marketed as a single product. The visible expression of the reproductive partnership manifesto.

- **For Her:** Egg quality, fertility support, methylfolate, CoQ10, myo-inositol, DHA. FSDU regulatory pathway.
- **For Him:** Sperm quality, motility, antioxidants, CoQ10, L-carnitine, zinc, selenium, ashwagandha (KSM-66). Health Supplement regulatory pathway.

**2. Edit Prenatal**
Comprehensive 18-25 ingredient prenatal supplement with branded traceable ingredients (Quatrefolic, life'sDHA, Albion chelated minerals). All within ICMR RDA. FSDU regulatory pathway.

### Year 1 expansion

**3. Edit Postnatal** (Month 9-12 post-launch)
Postpartum + lactation support, formulated for the mother's recovery and the breastfeeding period.

## Why Launch Couples Bundle + Prenatal at Day 1 (NOT All Three Together)

This was the most important strategic decision in the planning process. Three options were considered:

### Option A — Launch all 3 SKUs simultaneously
**Rejected.** Postnatal has no Day 1 customer demand. The natural Postnatal customer is your own Prenatal customer who just gave birth — and that cohort doesn't exist until Month 9-12 after launch. Manufacturing Postnatal at Day 1 means inventory sits in the warehouse for nearly a year before customers materialize, locking up capital and burning shelf life.

### Option B — Launch Prenatal only
**Rejected.** Misses the reproductive partnership positioning entirely. Pregnant women are already pregnant — they're past the partnership moment. Prenatal-only makes The Edit a maternity brand, not a partnership brand.

### Option C — Launch Couples Bundle + Prenatal at Day 1, add Postnatal at Month 9-12
**Selected.** This is the right architecture for four reasons:

1. **Captures the partnership positioning from Day 1.** Couples bundle is the hero. Prenatal is the natural progression. The brand's values are visible at launch, not deferred.

2. **Captures full female customer journey.** A woman who buys the Couples Bundle while trying to conceive transitions to Edit Prenatal when she gets pregnant — same brand, same trust, same subscription. She doesn't churn to a competitor at the most valuable moment.

3. **Postnatal launches at peak demand.** First Prenatal cohort gives birth at Month 9-12. Postnatal launches exactly when this cohort needs it. Capital efficient. Marketing efficient. Operationally clean.

4. **Two PR moments instead of one.** Day 1 launch (Couples Bundle + Prenatal) is the brand's manifesto moment. Month 9-12 Postnatal launch is the second wave — extends customer LTV and gives the brand a second cycle of media attention.

### The math comparison

| | All 3 Day 1 | Couples + Prenatal Day 1 | Prenatal only |
|---|---|---|---|
| Day 1 SKUs | 3 | 3 | 1 |
| Capital required at launch | Rs.25-30L | Rs.20-25L | Rs.10-12L |
| Build timeline | 18-24 months | 16-18 months | 12 months |
| Postnatal capital efficiency | **Bad** (sits unsold for 9-12 months) | Good (launches when demand exists) | N/A |
| Reproductive partnership lives at launch? | Yes | **Yes** | No |
| Customer journey coverage | Full | **Preconception → Pregnancy** | Pregnancy only |
| Execution risk for small team | **Very high** | Medium | Low |

The "Couples Bundle + Prenatal at Day 1" path captures 100% of the brand vision with significantly less capital, lower execution risk, and better inventory economics than launching all three together.

## Regulatory + Operational Foundation

**Operating entity:** Lumen Marketing Company (LMC) umbrella — saves Rs.30K + 4 weeks vs separate incorporation.

**Regulatory pathways:**
- FSDU (Foods for Special Dietary Use) — for Preconception Her + Prenatal + Postnatal
- Health Supplement — for Preconception Him

Both endorsements added to LMC's existing FSSAI Central License.

**Manufacturing:** India domestic only (rejected Thailand/overseas — contradicts brand thesis of "Indian women shouldn't have to import"). Currently evaluating Tanishq Lifecare (Ahmedabad) and Brukem Life Care (Ahmedabad) as contract manufacturers.

**Formulation philosophy:** All nutrients within ICMR RDA. Branded traceable ingredients (Quatrefolic for L-methylfolate, life'sDHA for algal omega-3, Albion for chelated minerals, KSM-66 for ashwagandha). No fillers. Nothing without published evidence.

## Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Indian men don't proactively buy supplements | Couples bundle ensures the woman buys for both; packaging makes his daily ritual frictionless |
| Cultural sensitivity around TTC framing | Target urban metro educated 28-38 demographic; don't try to appeal to all of India |
| Trimacare patent on trimester-specific MMN | Single comprehensive prenatal (like Ritual) sidesteps the patent |
| Manufacturer FSDU license gap | Verification questions sent to Tanishq + Brukem; if neither qualifies, pivot to alternatives in shortlist |
| Liability mixing under LMC umbrella | Future spin-out into separate Pvt Ltd when investment or scale demands |
| Gynecologist co-founder search | Step 0 of execution — the brand cannot launch without one |

## The Vision

The Edit is not a prenatal supplement company. It's a brand built around a single conviction: **the work of building a family is shared work.** Every product, every word, every design choice expresses this.

Day 1 launches three SKUs. Year 1 covers the full preconception-to-postnatal journey. Year 3-5 expands into adjacent reproductive wellness — fertility tracking, postpartum recovery, perimenopause, the menopausal transition. The Edit becomes a women's lifecycle wellness platform with the soul of reproductive partnership.

This is a Rs.500 Cr+ brand trajectory, not a Rs.50 Cr niche. The category is large. The white space is real. The brand vision is unique. The founder cares about this for reasons beyond commerce.

That's the proposal.
