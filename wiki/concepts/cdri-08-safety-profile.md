# CDRI-08 Safety Profile

The compiled safety package for [[cdri-08|CDRI-08]] / Mindwise. Sourced from [[2026-ctd-module-2.4-nonclinical-overview|CTD Module 2.4 (nonclinical)]] and [[2026-ctd-module-2.5-clinical-overview|CTD Module 2.5 (clinical)]]. **Every safety claim Mindwise makes must trace back to here.**

## Headline numbers

| Test | Result | Source |
|---|---|---|
| LD50 oral, rat | **>3000 mg/kg** | CDRI internal, Allan 2007 |
| LD50 oral, mouse | **>3000 mg/kg** | CDRI internal, Allan 2007 |
| LD50 IP, rat | 204.6 mg/kg | CDRI internal |
| LD50 IP, mouse | 224.6 mg/kg | CDRI internal |
| 14-day repeat oral, rat | NOAEL 250 mg/kg | Allan 2007 |
| 90-day subchronic, S-D rat | No toxicity at 500 mg/kg | Allan 2007 |
| 90-day subchronic, rhesus monkey | No side effects | CDRI 1996 |
| 13-week repeat dose, S-D rat | No toxicologically significant changes | CDRI Study GT14-00002 |
| Beagle dog dose escalation | Anorexia in females ≥62.5 mg/kg (transient) | CDRI Study GT14-00003 |
| Phase I human, 4-week | 20-300 mg single + 100-200 mg multiple, well tolerated | CDRI 1997 |

## Safety margin

LD50 oral > 3000 mg/kg in both rats and mice. Mindwise human dose = 300 mg/day = ~5 mg/kg for a 60 kg adult. **Safety margin = ~600× the daily human dose** before LD50.

## Genotoxicity (NEGATIVE — non-genotoxic)

- ✅ Ames bacterial reverse mutation: NEGATIVE (does not induce reverse mutation in *Salmonella typhimurium* or *E. coli*)
- ✅ In vitro mammalian chromosome aberration (CHO-K1 cells): NO chromosome aberrations
- ✅ In vivo bone marrow micronucleus (Swiss albino mice): not genotoxic
- ⚠️ Mildly clastogenic in human lymphocytes at 125 µg/mL **WITHOUT metabolic activation** — but **lost upon metabolic activation** (i.e., once liver enzymes process it, the effect disappears, which is what happens in vivo)
- **Conclusion:** non-genotoxic and safe at non-cytotoxic doses

## Carcinogenicity
**No data available.** This is a documented gap in the dossier. Does not preclude marketing but should not be claimed either way.

## Reproductive / Developmental toxicity

- Pregnant rats and rabbits during major organogenesis: **no teratogenic effects**
- ⚠️ **Singh 2009:** Bacopa 250 mg/kg in male mice over 28-56 days transiently suppressed spermatogenesis (sperm motility 56% of control, count 54%, viability 58%, abnormal sperm 2.6× increase). **All parameters normalized 56 days after treatment ended.** Libido and testosterone unaffected.
- Patel 2016: contradictory positive finding — improved sperm quality, spermatogenic cell density, steroidogenic indices in Parkes mice testes

> [!contradiction]
> Singh 2009 (negative) vs Patel 2016 (positive) on male fertility / spermatogenesis. Disclose both if asked. Likely dose- and species-dependent.

## Clinical adverse events (human)

- **Mild GI symptoms** (most common) — transient, self-resolving
- "Flu-like symptoms" and "digestive problems" — possibly related (per one trial)
- Thirst, nausea, muscle fatigue — reported in another study
- Phase I CDRI trial (4-week, healthy male volunteers): pharmacological doses 20-300 mg single + 100-200 mg multiple → well tolerated, no adverse effects

## Drug interactions to disclose

| Drug class | Direction | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Cholinergic drugs (e.g., dementia medications) | Theoretical: may enhance | Caution recommended |
| Phenytoin | Reverses phenytoin-induced cognitive impairment (positive) | Vohora 2000 |
| Morphine | Decreases toxicity of morphine (positive) | Abbas 2011 |
| Calcium channel blockers | Additive effect | Dar & Channa 1999 |
| CYP P450 metabolised drugs | Increases CYP P450 activity | Chowdhuri 2002 |
| Thyroid drugs (T4) | Increases thyroid hormone activity | Kar 2002 |
| Chlorpromazine class (antipsychotics) | **Negative interaction** — enhanced sedative effect | Ganguly & Malhotra 1967 |
| Acetylcholinesterase inhibitors | Theoretical risk of increased cholinergic side effects | Theoretical only |

## What this supports

✅ "40 years of safety data"
✅ "Proven safe at recommended dose"
✅ "Safety margin of >600× daily dose"
✅ "Non-genotoxic" (with the in-vivo caveat)
✅ "No teratogenic effects"
✅ "Well-tolerated in clinical trials"
✅ TGA approval (Australia) — safety dossier basis

## What it does NOT support

❌ "100% safe with no side effects" — mild GI events documented
❌ Claims about pregnancy / lactation safety in humans — only animal data
❌ Carcinogenicity claims either way (no data)
❌ Universally safe with all medications — disclose interaction list
