# CDRI-08

The standardised ethanolic extract of *Bacopa monnieri* (also written *Bacopa monniera*; common name: Brahmi) developed by [[csir-cdri|CSIR-CDRI Lucknow]]. **CDRI-08 is the active ingredient in [[domains/mindwise/_index|Mindwise]].** It is NOT generic Bacopa.

## What makes CDRI-08 different

| Attribute | CDRI-08 (Mindwise) | Generic Bacopa |
|---|---|---|
| Bacoside content | **55%+ combined bacosides A & B** (see [[55-percent-bacosides]]) | 20–30% |
| Origin | CSIR-CDRI patented extraction | Various, mostly unstandardised |
| Research backing | 40+ years preclinical + 7+ gold-standard human trials | Limited |
| Regulatory status | TGA-approved (Australia); CDRI government origin | Varies |
| Real-world history | Millions of users globally for 20+ years (KeenMind / MemoPlus Gold / etc.) | Varies |

This differentiation is the **entire commercial basis** for Mindwise. Conflating CDRI-08 with generic Bacopa kills the [[your-unfair-advantage|positioning]].

## From the Brand Brief (Jan 2026)

The [[brand-brief-jan-2026|foundational brief]] describes the 55% bacosides differentiation as the core scientific advantage: CDRI-08's bacoside concentration is nearly **double** the typical generic Bacopa extract. The brief also establishes the global track record: Philippines 20+ years (MemoPlus Gold, 15L+ capsules sold), Australia (KeenMind, TGA-approved). Product works from 1 week onwards, peak performance at 3 months. Mechanism described as "creates neurolinks, strengthens neurons."

## Mechanism (very abbreviated — see CTD for detail)

The principal constituents are dammarane-class triterpenoid saponins (bacosides A & B, bacopasides). Pharmacological activity is attributed to the saponins. Proposed mechanisms (none fully proven, all under investigation):

1. **Membrane dephosphorylation** + protein/RNA turnover increase in specific brain areas
2. **Cholinergic action** — reverses depletion of acetylcholine, increases choline acetylase activity
3. **Antioxidant effects** — comparable to common antioxidant agents in mode of action
4. **Hippocampal synaptic plasticity** — changes synapse properties relevant to memory formation
5. **Anti-inflammatory** — via betulinic acid component
6. **Anxiolytic** — possible serotonergic modulation
7. **Anti-amnesic** — reverses diazepam-induced amnesia (rat models)

Active form is likely the metabolites of the bacosides — the aglycones (jujubogenin, pseudojujubogenin) and derivatives (ebelin lactone, bacogenin A1) — which can cross the blood-brain barrier. Bacopaside I bioavailability in rats: 7–10%. (See [[cdri-08-pharmacology]] for the regulatory-grade detail.)

## Safety profile

See [[cdri-08-safety-profile]] for the regulatory-grade safety package. Headline numbers:
- LD50 oral (rats): >3000 mg/kg
- LD50 oral (mice): >3000 mg/kg
- 90-day subchronic oral: no toxicity at 500 mg/kg in S-D rats, in rhesus monkeys, or in 13-week S-D rat study
- Genotoxicity: not genotoxic (Ames negative, no chromosome aberrations in CHO cells under standard conditions)
- Reproductive: no teratogenic effects in rats or rabbits during organogenesis
- Adverse events seen in trials: mild GI symptoms (transient, self-resolving)
- One caveat: Singh 2009 reported reversible suppression of spermatogenesis in male mice at 250 mg/kg (parameters normalized after 56 days)
