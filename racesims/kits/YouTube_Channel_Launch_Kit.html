Step 5: YouTube Shorts Strategy

▶

Shorts are your fastest growth lever. Post 2-3 per week. Most can be repurposed from Instagram Reels.

Shorts Content Ideas (Ready to Film)

  • "Did you know?" — Quick race engineer facts. "Did you know F1 drivers brake with 150kg of force? Here's how load cell pedals replicate that."
  • Rig Build Timelapses — 30-60 second assembly clips with music. Already proven on IG.
  • Product Close-ups — Satisfying shots of hardware: wheel buttons, pedal mechanism, carbon fiber details.
  • Before/After — Desk setup → cockpit transformation reveal.
  • Reaction/Meme — "When someone says sim racing isn't real racing" + clip. Trending audio helps.
  • Quick Tips — "1 tip to improve your braking in 30 seconds" — screen recording with voiceover.
  • Unboxing Moments — Just the reveal moment from a full unboxing. Satisfying cuts.
Repurpose hack: Every Instagram Reel you post should also go on YouTube Shorts. Remove the IG watermark first (use SnapInsta or save original before posting to IG). Same video, new audience, zero extra effort.

Step 6: Thumbnail Best Practices

▶
  • Size: 1280x720px minimum, 16:9 aspect ratio
  • Text: Max 4-5 words in large, bold font. Must be readable on mobile.
  • Contrast: Dark background + bright text or vice versa. Red/white works with your brand.
  • Face: Thumbnails with faces get 30% higher CTR. Thambu's face + expression works.
  • Product shot: For gear reviews, show the product large and clear.
  • Consistency: Use same font, color scheme, layout style across all thumbnails. Builds brand recognition.
  • Tool: Use Canva (free) — search "YouTube Thumbnail" template and customize.
