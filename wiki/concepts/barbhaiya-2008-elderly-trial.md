# Barbhaiya et al. 2008 — Elderly Cognition Trial

Trial supporting [[cdri-08|CDRI-08]] efficacy in the **elderly cohort** — the third pillar (alongside [[stough-2008-90-day-trial|Stough 2008]] and [[roodenrys-2002-retention-trial|Roodenrys 2002]]) of the Mindwise efficacy evidence base for older adults.

## Citation

> Barbhaiya HC et al. (2008). Efficacy and tolerability of BacoMind on memory improvement in elderly participants: A double-blind placebo-controlled study. *J. Pharmacol. Toxicol.*, 3: 425-434.

## Study design

- **Format:** Double-blind, placebo-controlled, randomized, independent
- **Product:** BacoMind (a different commercial product also based on standardized Bacopa)
- **Dose:** 450 mg/day (single dose, high)
- **Cohort:** Elderly participants
- **Compliance:** 85%+ rate

## Findings

- ✅ **Improved cognition functions** — specifically attention and verbal memory in elderly
- Cognition improvement established for elderly population
- High dose (450 mg/day single dose) achieved measurable effects

## Quality rating
**Medium** — per CTD assessment:
- Cognition improvement established
- High single dose (450 mg/day) — note this is higher than Mindwise's standard 300 mg/day
- Low number of participants
- Some parameters statistically significant only at marginal level (close to p ≤ 0.05)

## Significance for Mindwise

This trial is **NOT directly on the Mindwise product** — it's on BacoMind, a different commercial Bacopa preparation. However:
- It IS standardized Bacopa monnieri extract
- The mechanism / active compound class is the same
- It's listed in Mindwise's [[2026-ctd-module-2.5-clinical-overview|CTD Module 2.5]] as supporting evidence

This means Mindwise can cite it as part of the **broader evidence base for Bacopa-based cognitive supplementation in elderly**, but should NOT cite it as a direct Mindwise trial.

## What it supports

✅ "Bacopa supplementation improves cognition in elderly populations" (general claim)
✅ Backing for the [[four-cohorts|aging cohort]] positioning
✅ Reference for "evidence base for elderly use"

❌ Should not be cited as "Mindwise trial" specifically
❌ The 450 mg dose should not be casually quoted — Mindwise's standard dose is 300 mg/day
