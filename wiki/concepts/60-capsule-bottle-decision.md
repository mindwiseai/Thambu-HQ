# 60-Capsule Bottle Decision

Locked in [[2026-03-17-meeting-binita-strategic-decisions|the March 17 meeting]]: the D2C SKU for [[domains/mindwise/_index|Mindwise]] is **60 capsules per bottle**, NOT 30.

## Rationale
- Target consumer is a high-performer who values cognitive edge over price sensitivity
- **Habit-building requires longer usage period** (2 months vs. 1 month)
- 60 caps better aligns with [[your-unfair-advantage|premium positioning]]
- Aligns with [[8-12-week-onset-claim|the 8-12 week onset window]] — a 30-cap bottle would deplete before peak results, undermining the science narrative

## Alternative SKU (B2B)
- 30-capsule **blister packs** continue for distributor / B2B channel
- Lower entry point for retail / pharmacy distribution
- Two blister-pack dimensions captured (per [[2026-03-23-binita-packaging-feedback]] suggestion #3): 60-bottle (70_10_120) and 120-bottle (115_75_125)

## Bottle sizing
- 60 capsules fit easily in 100mL bottle
- Mindwise capsules are very small vs. AceBlend (reference comparison: AceBlend ~150-200mL)
- Tested fit confirmed via samples sent in March
- Final dimensions per [[patco-pharmaceuticals|Patco]] manufacturing: 100ml rectangle, 48×48mm × 80mm height

## Dosing implication
- Adult dose: 2 capsules per day → 60 capsules = 30 days = ~1 month supply
- This means Mindwise should encourage **2-bottle minimum purchase** for first-time buyers (covers the 8-week onset window minimum)
- Bundle pricing structure should reflect this: e.g. buy 2 = 10% off, buy 3 = 15% off (per [[launch-plan-june-1-2026]] Phase 2)

⚠️ Note: child dose is **1 capsule/day** (per [[2026-03-23-binita-packaging-feedback]] feedback point 8) — so a kids-cohort 60-cap bottle = 2 months supply, very different LTV math.

## Why this matters for retention

The combination of:
- 60-cap bottle (1 month adult supply)
- 8-12 week onset window
- Premium price (~₹850-900)

means a buyer needs to commit to **2-3 bottles minimum** to feel the effect. The retention strategy must educate buyers about this upfront — otherwise they quit after 1 bottle and conclude "it didn't work."

This is why [[brain-health-community-vision|community-first content]] matters — it sets expectations the way packaging alone cannot.
