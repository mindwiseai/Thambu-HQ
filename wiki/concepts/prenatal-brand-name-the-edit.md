# The Edit — Prenatal Brand Name

**Status: candidate, pending trademark + domain verification** (2026-04-14)

[[aishwarya-chandrasekaran|Aishu's]] [[domains/prenatal/_index|prenatal brand]] is tentatively called **The Edit** — or possibly just **Edit**. Selected on 2026-04-14 after a naming exploration session that clarified the brand's true positioning.

## The positioning insight that led to this name

Aishu articulated the brand's core promise as:
> "Only what matters. Nothing else. No padding. No bullshit."

This is **essentialism**, not "precision" (exact measurement). It's the [[ritual-benchmark|Ritual]]-in-prenatal and The Ordinary-in-skincare positioning: *we took things out, that's the point*.

## Why The Edit fits

- **Name IS the promise** — "edit" literally means to remove the unnecessary
- **Premium DTC-coded** — same conceptual-minimalism lane as Everlane, Aesop, The Ordinary, Byredo
- **Extends cleanly** — The Prenatal Edit, The Postnatal Edit, The Fertility Edit, The Menopause Edit
- **Works for content** — brand journal, podcast, newsletter can all use "The Edit" umbrella
- **Differentiates from [[trimacare|Trimacare]]** — they throw everything in; Aishu strips it back
- **No existing prenatal supplement brand with this name found** in India, UK, or US searches (2026-04-14)

## Taglines that write themselves

- *"The prenatal edit. Everything earns its place."*
- *"We took things out. That's the point."*
- *"Only what matters during pregnancy. Nothing else."*

## Fallback naming options (if "The Edit" fails verification)

From the 2026-04-14 naming session, ordered by fit for the essentialism positioning:
- **Saar** (सार, Sanskrit: "essence, core, the point")
- **Only** (name as promise)
- **Matter** ("only what matters")
- **Mool** (मूल, Sanskrit: "root, fundamental")
- **Distill** (reduction to essence)

## Considered and rejected

- **Precision** — Aishu's initial pick. Rejected because (a) means "exact measurement" not "nothing extra," (b) trademark gridlock with Precision Nutrition Inc., (c) cold/clinical vibe conflicts with prenatal warmth, (d) reads as sports nutrition/male supplement
