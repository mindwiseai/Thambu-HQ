# Hero/Hub/Hygiene Content Framework

Content strategy framework adopted at [[2026-02-12-meeting-5-content-strategy|Meeting 07 — Content Strategy]] (February 12, 2026). Presented by [[aarti-samant]] and [[shipra]]. This is the structural backbone of the [[content-strategy-three-piece|three-piece content strategy]].

## The three tiers

### Hero content (big moments)
- Brand films, campaign launches, major collaborations
- High production value, emotional storytelling
- Drives awareness and brand building
- Example: [[learning-is-childs-play-campaign|"Learning is Child's Play"]] brand film, [[lead-the-room-campaign|"Lead the Room"]] musical
- Frequency: monthly or per campaign launch

### Hub content (community building)
- Educational series, expert interviews, community engagement
- Value-driven — 70-80% of Instagram content should be this (per [[aarti-samant]])
- Builds trust and gives people a reason to follow
- Instagram should act like a **creator, not a brand** (Neuro Gum cited as bad example of sales-heavy handle)
- Frequency: weekly

### Hygiene content (always-on)
- Tips, micro-explainers, reels, carousels, UGC-style
- Sustenance content that keeps the brand visible
- Targets behavior change (productivity habits, learning techniques)
- Creator partnerships (mom creators, productivity experts, doctors)
- Frequency: daily/every other day

## Key principle from Meeting 07

> Instagram content should be 70-80% value-driven, not selling. The brand personality is a **coach** who gamifies, gives tough love, is candid.

## Relationship to three-piece content strategy

The Hero/Hub/Hygiene framework maps directly to the [[content-strategy-three-piece]]:
- **Hero** = Brand Film (narrative)
- **Hub** = Key Visuals + educational content
- **Hygiene** = Sustenance / tips content
