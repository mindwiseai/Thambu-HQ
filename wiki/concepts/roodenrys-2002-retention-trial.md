# Roodenrys et al. 2002 — Memory Retention Trial

Critical secondary trial for the [[cdri-08|CDRI-08]] / Mindwise evidence base. Targets the **40-65 age cohort** — directly relevant to Mindwise's [[four-cohorts|"proactive agers"]] segment.

## Citation

> Roodenrys S, Booth D, Bulzomi S, Phipps A, Micallef C, Smoker J. (2002). Chronic effects of Brahmi (*Bacopa monniera*) on human memory. *Neuropsychopharmacology*, 27: 279–281.

## Study design

- **Format:** Double-blind, placebo-controlled
- **Sponsor:** Mindwise Pty Ltd
- **Product:** "Mindwise" — Standardized CDRI ethanolic Bacopa extract, 55%+ bacosides
- **Dose:** 300 mg/day for persons under 90 kg / **450 mg/day for persons over 90 kg**
- **Site:** Department of Psychology, University of Wollongong, Australia
- **Duration:** **6 weeks** + **3-month follow-up testing**
- **Participants:** 76 healthy volunteers (n=37 active, n=39 placebo)
- **Inclusion:** 40-65, healthy, informed consent
- **Exclusion:** medications, herbal preparations, head injuries

## Methodology
- Memory questionnaire
- General knowledge test
- Story recall
- Word pairs
- Digit span
- Visual span
- Coding
- Depression, Anxiety and Stress Scale (DASS)

## Findings

After 6 weeks:
- ✅ **Significantly improved retention of new information**
- 3-month follow-up: ✅ **Decreasing rate of forgetting** of newly acquired information

Tasks NOT significantly improved:
- ❌ Attention
- ❌ Verbal short-term memory
- ❌ Visual short-term memory
- ❌ Retrieval of pre-experimental knowledge

Wider areas (working memory, depression, anxiety, stress) — unaffected.

## Quality rating
**Medium** — per CTD assessment:
- Significant improvement in recall and retention
- No significant effect on short-term / working memory
- Medium number of participants
- Few methodological errors (no multiple-comparison adjustment)
- No systematic adverse-event documentation

## Significance for Mindwise

This is the trial Mindwise cites for:
- **Older-adult cohort relevance** — directly addresses 40-65 age range
- **Retention claim specifically** — more conservative than Stough but more directly applicable
- **3-month follow-up** — supports the [[8-12-week-onset-claim|sustained-effect over weeks]] story

The 450 mg/day dose for >90 kg users is also notable — it's the first study to suggest weight-based dosing variation. Mindwise has not adopted weight-based dosing in its current SKU.

## What it supports

✅ "Improves retention of new information" (older adults)
✅ "Decreases rate of forgetting"
✅ Older-adult cohort relevance ([[four-cohorts|"proactive agers"]])
❌ Don't claim short-term memory or attention improvements based on this trial alone — those were null
