# Startup Tax Exemption — FY2026

**RaceSims Solutions Pvt Ltd is using its Indian startup certification income-tax exemption in FY2026.** Surfaced in the [[2026-03-26-thambu-voice-brief|March 26 voice brief]].

## The exemption (Section 80-IAC, India)

India's Startup India initiative provides eligible DPIIT-recognized startups with a **100% income tax exemption for any 3 consecutive financial years out of the first 10 years** since incorporation. Key facts:

- **Duration:** 3 consecutive FYs, chosen by the startup
- **Window:** must fall within first 10 years of incorporation
- **Qualification:** DPIIT recognition + turnover under ₹100 cr in the chosen years + innovation-based business (RaceSims qualifies on all counts)
- **Choosable timing:** the company picks WHEN to claim the 3 years (i.e., strategically time against expected profit years)

Per the voice brief:
> "For any three years, we can use it; we are going ahead and using it this year."

So **FY2026 is year 1 of 3** for RaceSims' exemption claim.

## Why this creates year-end urgency

To maximize the exemption value, RaceSims wants **as much profit as possible** in FY2026 (since it won't be taxed). Thambu's year-end push has several drivers:

| Task | Why it matters for the exemption |
|---|---|
| **Reconcile [Zoho Books](https://www.zoho.com/in/books/)** | Clean books = accurate profit calculation = clean exemption claim |
| **Bill and close remaining motion system sale** | Revenue booked in FY2026 = tax-free profit |
| **Close maximum sales before year-end** | Same — more top line in the exempt year |
| **Year-end Coimbatore / Bangalore / Mumbai trips** | Each trip has revenue-generating tasks (dealer coordination, installs, customer deliveries) |

Every closed sale before March 31 2026 is a **tax-free rupee**. The incentive is direct.

## Strategic implications

1. **Accelerate revenue into FY2026.** Bill now. Ship now. Close now. The 12 months after are not as valuable per rupee.
2. **Defer expenses to FY2027.** If there's a choice between booking an expense now vs next year, defer it (expenses don't reduce tax-free income, so they're "worth less" in an exemption year).
3. **Bundle trips for efficiency.** Thambu's 4-city trip plan (Chennai → Coimbatore → Bangalore → Mumbai) is partly tax-driven — each city has revenue tasks that need closing before year-end.
4. **The [[2026-03-26-thambu-voice-brief|March 26 voice brief]] itself is a year-end rush plan**, not a normal-pace task list.

## The 3-year strategic choice

If FY2026 is year 1, Thambu must also think about:
- **FY2027, FY2028 as years 2 and 3?** — The safe default (consecutive years are required).
- **Growth trajectory:** ideally RaceSims grows each year so profits get larger in years 2 and 3 = more exemption value captured.
- **Post-2028:** regular corporate tax rates apply. Plan accordingly.
