## CRITICAL STRATEGIC INSIGHTS

### **Insight 1: The "Scary Drug" Problem**
When Sorted shared sample bottles internally, people asked: "Can I even have this? Should a doctor tell me?"  
→ Label was over-informative, making it feel prescription-required

**Solution:** Semi-transparent bottle removes fear by showing actual product + minimal label copy

### **Insight 2: The Age Paradox**
Brand must appeal to 18-year-old students AND 80-year-old seniors simultaneously  
→ Can't be too trendy (alienates elders) or too clinical (alienates youth)

**Solution:** "Unfair Advantage" language + modern-but-professional aesthetic works across ages

### **Insight 3: Science as Secondary**
Consumers want to feel the brand is credible, but don't want scientific information ON the package  
→ Too much copy = too clinical = too scary

**Solution:** Design communicates "expert + modern + performance" visually; website delivers science

### **Insight 4: Content as Community Building**
Not just selling supplement; teaching brain health as lifestyle  
→ Creates brand advocates, not just customers

**Solution:** Mix of brand films (narrative) + sustenance content (tips, habits, facts)

### **Insight 5: Green Capsules as Asset**
The actual product (green capsules) is visually distinctive and poppy  
→ Current packaging hid this natural differentiator

**Solution:** Semi-transparent bottle showcases the product itself as design element
