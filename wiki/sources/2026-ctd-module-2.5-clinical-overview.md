# CTD Module 2.5 — Clinical Overview

ICH Common Technical Document Module 2 — Clinical Overview for the Mindwise / [[cdri-08]] dossier. This is the **single most load-bearing regulatory document** for everything Mindwise claims clinically. Every marketing claim about memory, focus, attention, or cognitive function must trace back to a study referenced here.

## Key clinical trials documented (the cite-able evidence base)

| Trial | Year | Cohort | Duration | Primary finding |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [[stough-2001-12-week-trial]] | 2001 | Adults 18-60 | 12 weeks | Visual info processing, learning rate, memory consolidation, anxiety reduction |
| [[roodenrys-2002-retention-trial]] | 2002 | Adults 40-65 | 6 weeks (+3-month follow-up) | Improved retention of new information; decreased forgetting |
| [[stough-2008-90-day-trial]] | 2008 | Adults 18-60 | 90 days | Improved spatial working memory accuracy + reduced false positives in RVIP |
| [[barbhaiya-2008-elderly-trial]] | 2008 | Elderly | — | Cognition (attention, verbal memory) improvements |
| Calabrese et al. 2008 | 2008 | Elderly | — | Multiple measures of cognitive performance + anxiety + depression |
| Morgan & Stevens 2010 | 2010 | Older adults | — | Memory acquisition (visuospatial null) |
| Mandal et al. 2011 | 2011 | Adults | — | Cognition improvement (450 mg/day single dose) |

The Pase et al. 2011 systematic review concluded these effects are evident **at three months**, not earlier — direct backing for the [[8-12-week-onset-claim]].

## Active ingredient (consistently across all trials)

> **"Standardized ethanolic Bacopa monniera extract (CDRI, Lucknow, India) for Bacosides A and B (no less than 55% of combined bacosides)"**

This is the regulatory grounding for the [[55-percent-bacosides|55%+ bacosides]] claim that anchors the entire Mindwise differentiation.

## Mechanism summary (per 2.7.2)

Pharmacological actions documented:
- Analgesic, anthelmintic, antifungal, antioxidant, anti-stress, anti-ulcer, anti-inflammatory, hepatoprotective, immunological, mast cell stabilizing, muscular, neurological, **nootropic**, sedative, cardiovascular

Proposed mechanisms for cognitive effects:
1. Bacosides induce membrane dephosphorylation → increased protein and RNA turnover in specific brain areas
2. Enhanced protein kinase activity in hippocampus → repair of damaged neurons
3. Cholinergic action (reverses acetylcholine depletion)
4. Antioxidant effects (comparable to common antioxidant agents)
5. Anti-inflammatory effects (betulinic acid)
6. Possible serotonergic modulation (anxiolytic effect)

Most studies showed **no significant physiological, biochemical, or electrocardiographic adverse effects** at recommended oral dosage in any test organism (including human).

## Safety summary (per 2.7.4)

- LD50 oral (rats): >3000 mg/kg
- LD50 oral (mice): >3000 mg/kg
- LD50 IP (rats): 204.6 mg/kg
- LD50 IP (mice): 224.6 mg/kg
- 90-day subchronic + 13-week S-D rat toxicity: no toxicity at 500 mg/kg
- Beagle dog dose escalation: anorexia in females at 250-500 mg/kg
- 4-week double-blind in healthy male volunteers: 20-300 mg single + 100-200 mg multiple = well-tolerated
- Clinical adverse events: mild GI symptoms (transient, self-resolving)
- Possible interactions: cholinergic drugs (theoretical), phenytoin (Bacopa reverses phenytoin-induced cognitive impairment), morphine (Bacopa decreases toxicity), chlorpromazine class (negative interaction documented)

## What this document supports

✅ "55% bacosides" claim ([[55-percent-bacosides]])
✅ "7+ clinical trials" claim
✅ "Improves working memory" — specifically Stough 2008
✅ "Improves information processing speed" — Stough 2001
✅ "Improves retention of new information" — Roodenrys 2002
✅ "8-12 weeks to peak results" — Pase 2011 systematic review
✅ "Government-backed" — CDRI is the developer / patent holder
✅ "Reduces anxiety" — limited support, Stough 2001
✅ TGA approval (Australia)

## What this document does NOT support
❌ Treatment claims for ADHD, autism, dementia, Alzheimer's
❌ "Reverses brain damage"
❌ "Instant" or "fast-acting" cognitive effects
❌ Anti-aging claims
❌ Disease prevention claims
