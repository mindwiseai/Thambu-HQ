# 55%+ Bacosides — The Core Differentiator

**The single most important quantitative claim that distinguishes [[domains/mindwise/_index|Mindwise]] from generic Bacopa.** Backed by [[csir-cdri|CSIR-CDRI]]'s patented extraction process.

## The claim

| Product | Bacoside content |
|---|---|
| **[[cdri-08|Mindwise (CDRI-08)]]** | **No less than 55% combined Bacosides A & B** |
| Generic Bacopa supplements | 20–30% bacosides (typical) |
| [[placebo-wellness-enemy|"Placebo Wellness" tier]] | Often unspecified or unmeasured |

## Why it matters

The bacosides (and related bacopasides) are the **bioactive constituents** responsible for the cognitive and neuropharmacological effects of *Bacopa monnieri*. Higher concentration = higher pharmacological dose at the same capsule mass.

A 160 mg dose at 55% bacosides delivers ~88 mg of active. The same dose at 25% delivers ~40 mg. **More than 2× the active.**

This is why:
- The Mindwise dosing instructions match the clinical trial dosing (300 mg/day for adults = 2 × 150 mg capsules — see [[stough-2008-90-day-trial]])
- The trials backing Mindwise can be cited *as if they were Mindwise's own*: they used the same standardized extract
- Generic Bacopa supplements **cannot** legitimately claim the same trial backing

Per [[2026-03-23-binita-packaging-feedback]] feedback point 5, the proposed back-of-pack copy (Slide 11) should sharpen the differentiation:

> ❌ "Clinical gold standard is 50% bacosides" (current — too vague)
>
> ✅ **"Most Bacopa supplements contain only 20-30% bacosides. Mindwise delivers a clinically optimized 55%."** (proposed — concrete, comparison-driven)

This direct comparison is much sharper because it gives the reader an actual contrast number.

## Regulatory grounding

The 55%+ bacoside claim is backed by:
- [[csir-cdri|CDRI]] internal extraction standards
- TGA (Australia) regulatory approval (which audits this directly)
- Multiple clinical trial documents specifying "no less than 55% of combined bacosides" — see [[stough-2008-90-day-trial]], [[stough-2001-12-week-trial]], [[roodenrys-2002-retention-trial]]
- Full [[2026-ctd-module-2.5-clinical-overview|CTD Module 2.5]] documentation
