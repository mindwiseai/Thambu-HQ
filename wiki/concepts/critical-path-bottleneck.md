# Critical Path Bottleneck

The single most important risk in the [[launch-plan-june-1-2026|June 1 Mindwise launch]]. Flagged explicitly in the launch plan source doc.

## The bottleneck

> ⚠️ **The visual identity presentation (due by April 2) unlocks everything downstream — website design, packaging finalisation, social media content, and marketing collateral. Any delay here cascades across all workstreams.**

## The dependency chain

```
Visual Identity Presentation (April 2)
        ↓
Brand Book + Design Language LOCKED (April 10)
        ↓
        ├── Website design approved (April 15) → Dev (April 15-May 15) → QA (May 7-18) → Soft launch (May 29) → LAUNCH June 1
        ├── Packaging artwork → Patco production (April 25 sample → May 1 production → May 15 inventory)
        ├── Social media templates → Daily content production (April 25-May 7)
        └── Performance ad creatives (May 18-25)
```

If visual identity slips by even one week past April 10 design lock, **the entire downstream slides**. There is essentially no slack.

## Why so little slack?

The launch plan is **already** working backwards from a fixed June 1 date. Sorted's website timeline alone is 30-35 days of development, plus QA, plus soft launch, plus launch week. That's ~50 days. Packaging needs 4-6 weeks lead time at Patco. Both run in parallel from the April 10 design lock.

50 days of fixed downstream + April 10 lock = June 1 launch. **Any earlier delay propagates 1:1.**

## The escalation path

Per launch plan risk register:
> "Mitigation: Agree on hard deadline with Sorted. **If missed, escalate to Aarti**."

Aarti owns the relationship at Sorted level. Manik runs day-to-day. Escalation to Aarti = relationship-level intervention, not project-level nudging.

## Fallback: June 15 launch (the formal slip)

The launch plan explicitly defines May 25 as **GO/NO-GO** day. If critical blockers exist on May 25, the formal fallback is **June 15** — 2 weeks slip, not a full month. This protects against cascading partial readiness while keeping the launch in a defined window.
