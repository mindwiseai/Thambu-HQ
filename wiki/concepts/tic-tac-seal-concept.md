# Tic Tac Seal Concept

A packaging UX exploration for [[domains/mindwise/_index|Mindwise]]: a **partial-opening seal** mechanism that lets capsules come out one at a time (like Tic Tac mints), instead of spilling out when the bottle tilts.

Flagged in [[2026-03-23-binita-packaging-feedback|the March 23 feedback to Binita]]:

> "As discussed, we're exploring the partial-opening seal idea (like Tic Tac — capsules come out one at a time instead of spilling). Even if it's marked as 'design exploration / pending manufacturer confirmation,' it should be in the brief so it's on everyone's radar."

## Why explore this

- Premium UX feel — interaction with the bottle becomes part of the daily ritual
- Reinforces the [[your-unfair-advantage|"daily habit"]] frame (vs. supplement bottles that spill)
- Differentiates from the [[placebo-wellness-enemy|generic Bacopa]] category which uses standard caps
- Single-capsule dispensing matches the [[8-12-week-onset-claim|"this is a habit"]] education narrative

## Constraints
- Must be confirmed feasible by [[patco-pharmaceuticals|Patco]] or alternative manufacturer
- Must work with the [[60-capsule-bottle-decision|60-capsule]] format
- Must not compromise the [[semi-transparent-bottle|semi-transparent visual aesthetic]] (no opaque cap dome that hides the green capsules)
- Must be cost-feasible for launch volumes

## Status
- ⏳ Design exploration only
- ⏳ Pending manufacturer confirmation
- Should appear in the next Sorted packaging brief deck revision

## Risk if it doesn't work
- Fall back to standard cap → no UX innovation, but doesn't break anything else
- Track 2 ([[two-track-bottle-strategy|realistic backup]]) already assumes a standard cap
