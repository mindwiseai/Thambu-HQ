## The shortlist

These are the subtitles that resonated from the brainstorm session. Listed in the order Aishu chose them, with light cleanup of voice-typing artifacts.

### 1. The Edit. **The Pre-Baby Shelf.**
*Editorial, ownable, casual enough that real people would say it. "Shelf" implies curation — fits the essentialism positioning.*

### 2. The Edit. **Wellness, edited down.**
*Self-referential, smart. Reinforces both the brand name AND the essentialism. Doesn't lean on couples — couples comes through in the product.*

### 3. The Edit. **We took things out. On purpose.**
*Has personality. The most direct articulation of the essentialism manifesto. A bit longer — works better in headlines than packaging.*

### 4. The Edit. **A thoughtful start.**
*Quiet luxury, Aesop-coded. Premium, broadly likeable. The most "calm confidence" of the bunch.*

### 5. The Edit. **Daily. Deliberate. Together.**
*Three-beat rhythm. Captures essentialism + couples + ritual in 4 words. Works in voiceover, packaging, and social.*

### 6. The Edit. **The thinking person's prenatal.**
*Bold, opinionated. Says: "this isn't for everyone, and we're okay with that." Filters customers — the right ones lean in.*

### 7. The Edit. **The biology of beginning.**
*Body-truth + warmth. Modern science, lyrically told. Could extend across all SKUs (preconception, prenatal, postnatal — all "biology of beginning").*

### 8. **Edited.**
*The standalone option. Brand as verb. The most stripped-back version possible. Bold choice — the brand IS the subtitle.*

### 9. The Edit. **For the year that changes everything.**
*Time-coded, emotional, specific. "The year" = the year of trying + pregnancy. Universal pregnancy truth told in brand voice.*

---

## Quick observations on the shortlist

Looking at the 9 picks together, a pattern emerges. Almost all of them lean **essentialist + thoughtful + quietly confident** rather than **loud / cheeky / opinionated**. That's a useful signal — the brand voice you're gravitating toward is **considered, premium, and confident** rather than activist or comedic.

The exceptions are #6 (The thinking person's prenatal) and #3 (We took things out. On purpose.) — both have more edge. Worth noting whether those felt like outliers you'd *use*, or outliers you *liked but wouldn't use*.

## Suggested next steps

When you're ready to move from "shortlist" to "lock":

1. **Pick top 3.** Live with them for a few days. Say each one out loud in a sentence: *"I take The Edit. [Subtitle]"* — see which one feels least awkward.
2. **Trademark check.** For the top 3, search IP India in Class 5 (supplements) + Class 30 (food) for the full phrase.
3. **Tagline test.** Try writing 1 founder's letter and 1 Instagram caption using each. The one that's easiest to write copy for usually wins.
4. **Visual test.** Mock up a packaging label with each subtitle. The one that looks RIGHT (proportions, rhythm, breathing room) usually wins.
