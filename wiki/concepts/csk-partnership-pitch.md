# CSK Partnership Pitch Strategy

The strategic framing of [[thambu]]'s outreach to [[chennai-super-kings|Chennai Super Kings]] ([[2026-04-08-csk-pitch-draft|pitch details]]). This concept page captures **why** the pitch is structured the way it is, what it's trying to accomplish, and how it fits into the broader RaceSims strategy — separate from the tactical artifacts (email body, DM text) on the source page.

## The pitch in one sentence

**"Put CSK players behind a sim-racing rig for free, in exchange for the content their reactions create."**

## The structural logic (why this is a good pitch)

1. **Zero-cost offer.** Lowers the "should I even reply?" friction for the CSK gatekeeper. No budget approval required.
2. **Four formats, not one.** Demonstrates content portfolio thinking, not a single-idea pitch. Shows RaceSims has thought about this seriously.
3. **Honest asymmetric framing.** Thambu explicitly says *"we're not pretending this is an equal trade — the exposure we'd get from being associated with CSK would be huge for us."* This disarms the cynicism that CSK's team develops from seeing bad pitches every week.
4. **Serialized content design.** The Lap Time Challenge and Fan Challenge formats are specifically designed to come back week after week, not one-off. Matches CSK's content cadence.
5. **Small ask.** "10 minutes to walk you through it" — not "let's schedule a full meeting" or "give me 30 min." Minimum viable friction.
6. **Two-channel delivery** (Instagram DM + Email). Redundancy against either channel being missed or filtered.
7. **Local Chennai angle.** Thambu is Chennai-based; CSK is Chennai-based. Geographic proximity makes logistics trivial and the narrative "local founder pitches local team" is press-friendly.

## The four content formats (archetypes covered)

| Archetype | Format | CSK content calendar fit |
|---|---|---|
| Entertainment / reactions | **Player vs Player** head-to-head | Standard Reels content, high engagement potential |
| Serialized / recurring | **Lap Time Challenge** leaderboard | Fills a recurring slot — "Tuesday = new lap time update" |
| Behind-the-scenes | **Guess the Driver** blindfolded | Plays on team camaraderie, fits existing CSK BTS content |
| UGC / fan activation | **Fan Challenge** (fan-submitted laps) | CRM + community-building, with a player-meeting prize |

This isn't a random list — each format hits a different content-marketing archetype. Together they cover the full spectrum of what a modern IPL team's social team wants.

## What RaceSims gets (the honest trade)

Per Thambu's own framing:
- **Massive exposure** (CSK has millions of followers across platforms)
- **Credibility uplift** — "partners with CSK" is the press line
- **Content library** — Reels and clips RaceSims can reuse on its own channels
- **Press story** — [[yourstory|YourStory]] / Inc42 pitch material (the single biggest RaceSims PR gap flagged in [[backlink-strategy]])
- **Funnel into future IPL team pitches** — once one IPL team runs, pitching MI, RCB, KKR becomes trivial
- **Validation** for the [[race-engineer-positioning|race-engineer moat]] in a premium sports context
- **Potential physical event** (stadium fan zone with sim rigs) — longer-term upside

## What CSK gets (the modest trade)

- **Fresh content format** that no IPL team has done
- **Free equipment + setup + filming support** (all costs on RaceSims)
- **New Reels series** with minimal production overhead
- **Low risk** — one session, no ongoing commitment, can drop if it doesn't perform
