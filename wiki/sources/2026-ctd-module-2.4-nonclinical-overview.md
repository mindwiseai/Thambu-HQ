## Key headlines

### Pharmacology (mechanism)

The Bacopa monnieri herb has been used as a therapeutic agent for memory improvement and cognitive function for centuries in Ayurvedic medicine. Animal studies (mice, rats, rabbit, beagle dog, monkey) document neuropharmacological effects via:

- **Cholinergic system** — reverses acetylcholine depletion, increases choline acetylase, increases muscarinic receptor binding (against neurotoxin / colchicine damage)
- **Hippocampal synaptic plasticity** — changes properties of hippocampal synapses relevant to memory formation
- **Anxiolytic** — comparable to lorazepam in high oral doses (clinically validated rat anxiety model)
- **Antioxidant** — comparable to common antioxidant agents
- **Anti-inflammatory** — via betulinic acid
- **Mast cell stabilization**
- **Anti-amnesic** — reverses diazepam-induced amnesia in rats

### Active form

Per Ramasamy et al. 2015 — through in silico + in vitro experiments, the **metabolites** of bacosides (aglycones: jujubogenin, pseudojujubogenin; derivatives: ebelin lactone, bacogenin A1) are more likely the active form, NOT the parent bacosides themselves. These metabolites are absorbed via intestines and can cross the blood-brain barrier.

### Pharmacokinetics

- Few studies (Ramasamy 2014, Bhateria 2016, Yin 2016)
- Parent glycosides not found in plasma after oral dosing — only metabolites
- Poor intestinal absorption of glycosides due to low membrane permeability
- Bacopaside I oral bioavailability in rats: **7.22% / 9.69% / 8.30%** at 3 / 6 / 12 mg/kg
- Cmax within 22 minutes; t½ 50-80 minutes
- Bhateria 2016: bacopaside I IV in mice → confirmed BBB crossing
- CYP3A and P-glycoprotein effects studied in S-D rats

### Toxicology — single dose

| Species | Route | LD50 |
|---|---|---|
| Wistar rat | oral | >3000 mg/kg |
| Wistar rat | IP | 204.6 mg/kg |
| Mouse | oral | >3000 mg/kg |
| Mouse | IP | 224.6 mg/kg |

CDRI-08 has a **high safety margin** (LD50 oral >3000 mg/kg). At 500 mg/kg p.o.: no neuronal deficit, no hypo/hyperthermia, no diuretic effects, no anti-inflammatory effect, no hypo/hypertensive effects.

### Toxicology — repeated dose

- 14-day repeat (S-D rats, 250/500/1000 mg/kg): toxicity signs (writhing) at 1000 mg/kg in both sexes; mild lowering of body weight in males at ≥500 mg/kg; **no abnormal effects at 250 mg/kg**
- 90-day subchronic (S-D rats): no toxicity at 0.85, 210, 500 mg/kg
- 90-day study (rhesus monkeys): no side effects
- **13-week** repeated oral dose (S-D rats): no abnormal lesions, no toxicologically significant changes
- Beagle dogs (dose escalation): anorexia in female at 62.5 / 250 / 500 mg/kg; transient

### Genotoxicity

- **Chromosomal aberration (CHO cells):** No aberrations under standard conditions
- **Ames bacterial reverse mutation:** Negative
- **Micronucleus (mouse bone marrow, in vivo):** Not genotoxic
- Mildly clastogenic effect at 125 µg/mL in human lymphocytes **without metabolic activation**, but **lost upon metabolic activation** — therefore non-genotoxic in vivo

**Conclusion:** non-genotoxic and safe for human consumption at non-cytotoxic doses.

### Carcinogenicity
**No data available.** This is a documented gap in the dossier.

### Reproductive / Developmental toxicity

- Pregnant rats (Charles Foster, 50/100 mg/kg/day, days 6-15) and rabbits (NZW, 26.66/53.32 mg/kg/day, days 6-18) during major organogenesis
- All animals remained healthy; no mortality; no obvious change or sign of reaction
- **No teratogenic effects** linked to nature or dose of CDRI-08
- ⚠️ Singh 2009: Bacopa at 250 mg/kg in male mice over 28-56 days **transiently suppressed spermatogenesis** (sperm motility 56% of control, count 54%, viability 58%, abnormal sperm 2.6× increase). **Reversible** — all parameters normalized 56 days after treatment ended. Libido and testosterone unaffected.
- ⚠️ Note: this is a real adverse finding to disclose if asked, even though it was reversible
- Patel 2016: contradictory positive finding — CDRI-08 improved sperm quality, spermatogenic cell density, and steroidogenic indices in Parkes mice testes

> [!contradiction]
> Singh 2009 and Patel 2016 disagree on Bacopa's effect on male fertility / spermatogenesis. Both studies should be disclosed to anyone asking. Resolve by noting that effects appear dose-dependent and species-dependent, and that Mindwise dosing is well below either study's range.
