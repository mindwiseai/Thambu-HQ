## Logo emotion deep dive

**Current logo issues identified:**
- Generic, archaic, gets lost in crowd, forgettable, mundane, dull colors, templatized appearance, doesn't evoke performance/growth mindset

**What logo MUST communicate:**
- Expert-driven (vs. generic)
- Performance-oriented
- New-age / modern
- Not scary / prescription
- Approachable + aspirational
- Works for 18-year-olds AND seniors ([[age-paradox]])
- Confident but not arrogant

**Design philosophy:** *"Everything is emotion right now — marketing is emotion in a lot of ways."* Remove all context (no packaging, no website) — what does logo alone feel like?

### Visual identity decision framework
- **Heights brief:** Maximum differentiation
- **AG1 brief:** Minimalist sophistication
- **Whoop brief:** Tech-backed authority
- **Mindwise direction:** Hybrid — cleaner than current, aspirational like AG1/Whoop, leverage unique green capsule asset like Über

## ⭐ THE GREEN CAPSULE INSIGHT (the unlock)

**Major insight:** the green capsules are poppy/eye-catching:
- Should be visible/celebrated, not hidden
- Semi-transparent bottle reveals the visual differentiator
- Simple execution: transparent bottle + frosted sleeve + green showing through
- Not complicated to produce
- Creates natural intrigue: *"What is this brand?"* curiosity

## ⭐ COLOR SCHEME LOCKED

**Monochrome language + green pop:**
- Not colorful, not vibrant
- Not doing 10 things simultaneously
- Monochrome foundation: green, black, gray, or blue
- **Recommended:** Charcoal/blackish-gray + GREEN POP

**Practical execution:**
- Transparent bottle (standard shape, cost-controlled)
- Frosted/matte sleeve with design elements
- Green capsules visible inside creating color pop
- Logo likely dark/monochrome
- Overall feel: premium, modern, tech-forward

## Design aesthetic goals
- Performance-driven (athlete/optimizer mindset)
- New tech (modern, not retro)
- Educational (backs message with clarity)
- Clean (not cluttered)
- Aspirational ("I want this")
- Approachable ("not scary")
- Age-agnostic (works for 18-80 — see [[age-paradox]])

## Packaging philosophy: Less = More

**Current problem:** Too much information on bottle
**Solution direction:** Information hierarchy — bottle catches eye, website answers questions

- **Bottle:** Visual appeal, minimal copy, call-to-action
- **Website:** Full story, science, credibility, testimonials
- **Modern consumer:** Google search gets information freely; packaging should intrigue, not educate

> "When someone picks it up in a store or sees it online, they get intrigued and go to the website. Information not needed on bottle itself."

## Decisions LOCKED in this meeting
- ⭐ [[semi-transparent-bottle]] — hero design choice (the single decision that solved the [[scary-drug-problem]])
- ⭐ Monochrome + green pop color scheme
- Modern, tech-forward, performance-driven aesthetic locked
- Information hierarchy: minimal on bottle, full story on website
- Logo design route principles (Route 3 specifics came later in [[2026-03-17-meeting-binita-strategic-decisions|Binita meeting]])
