# Prenatal Brand India Strategy

Comprehensive strategy document compiled from [[aishwarya-chandrasekhar|Aishu's]] conversations with Claude. Covers market research, competitive analysis, regulatory framework, manufacturer evaluation, and launch roadmap for India's first transparency-led DTC prenatal supplement brand.

## Summary

The document captures the full strategic foundation for a prenatal/postnatal supplement brand modeled on [[ritual-benchmark|Ritual]] (US). Key components: FSDU regulatory path (not Health Supplement), gynecologist co-founder requirement, DTC subscription model, lifestyle branding over pharma-coded approach, and a phased clinical proof strategy.

## Key facts

- Market opportunity: $164B Indian wellness market growing at 5.14% CAGR
- [[trimacare]] is main competitor but pharma-coded, doctor-driven — no Indian brand does Ritual-style DTC
- [[brukem-life-care]] is top-pick contract manufacturer (Ahmedabad, USFDA, prenatal experience)
- Product must register as FSDU under FSSAI — Health Supplement category would require "not for pregnant women" warning
- All nutrients must stay within ICMR RDA or product triggers CDSCO drug classification
- Pricing target: Rs.800-1,500/month (premium, urban metros initially)
- Launch timeline: ~6 months from gynecologist co-founder secured

## People mentioned

- [[aishwarya-chandrasekhar]] — founder, compiled this research
- [[thambu]] — co-founder, experienced the trust gap personally during pregnancy

## Decisions

- **LOCKED:** Lifestyle brand with clinical backing (not pharma-coded)
- **LOCKED:** FSDU regulatory path
- **LOCKED:** DTC subscription as primary channel
- **LOCKED:** Gynecologist must be co-founder level
- **PENDING:** Gynecologist co-founder search
- **PENDING:** Manufacturer selection (Brukem top pick)
- **PENDING:** Brand name, identity, packaging
