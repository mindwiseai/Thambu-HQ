Technical Questions

What is the difference between a direct drive and belt-drive wheel?

A belt-drive wheel uses belts and gears between the motor and the steering shaft, which adds some friction, noise, and slight delay to the force feedback. A direct drive wheel connects the motor directly to the steering shaft with no intermediary mechanism. This gives you instant, precise force feedback with more detail — you can feel every bump, slide, and weight transfer. For serious sim racing and especially for driver training, direct drive is strongly recommended. While entry-level direct drive options exist from brands like Moza, as a race engineer I recommend investing in at least an 8Nm setup to get the feedback detail that actually develops your driving skills.

What are load cell pedals and are they worth it?

Standard potentiometer pedals measure brake input by how far you push the pedal (distance). Load cell pedals measure how hard you press (force/pressure), which is exactly how real racing car brake pedals work. This makes your braking far more consistent and precise. As a race engineer, I consider load cell pedals the single most impactful upgrade for lap time improvement. The difference in braking consistency is immediately measurable in telemetry data. Conspit offers some of the best load cell and hydraulic pedals available in India through RaceSims — their progressive brake feel is as close to a real race car as sim hardware gets.

Do I need a cockpit/rig or can I use a desk mount?

You can start with a desk mount, but a proper cockpit or rig is strongly recommended for any serious sim racing. Here's why: a desk mount flexes under force feedback, which reduces immersion and makes your inputs less precise. A rigid cockpit keeps everything locked in place, just like a real race car.
