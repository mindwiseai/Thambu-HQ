## The diagnosis: [[scary-drug-problem]]

When [[sorted-agency|Sorted]] shared sample bottles with their internal team:
- **Initial reaction:** confusion — *"Can I even have this? Should a doctor tell me?"*
- **Perception issue:** too clinical, medicinal, cluttered, confusing
- **Fear-inducing aspect:** over-information on label suggesting prescription requirement
- **Social media reflection:** current posts echo this cluttered clinical tone — needs complete shift

## The strategic reframe

**Current issue:** Looks like something that needs doctor approval (too scary)
**Target shift:** Make it feel like something you're missing out on if you don't have it (FOMO + aspiration)

Not a "scary drug" but a "formal drug":
- Scary = needs prescription, medical condition response
- Formal = professional, research-backed, BUT for optimization and performance

## ⭐ THE BRAND MANIFESTO LOCKED

> # **"Your Unfair Advantage"**

This single positioning does three things:
1. **Not magical** — tells people this isn't a gimmick
2. **Work done** — implies 40+ years research already invested
3. **Level up** — unlocks potential and performance

The emotion: *"I have leverage in the world by taking this"*

See [[your-unfair-advantage]] for full unpacking.

## The "Unfair Advantage" as differentiator
- Moves from clinical/technical communication → softer, higher-order leverage narrative
- Target: students to professionals to seniors all understand this language
- **Logo challenge:** must cut across ages (appeal to 18-year-old AND 80-year-old) — see [[age-paradox]]
- **Tone shift:** Expert-driven (we know our shit, 40 years research) but NOT government body (not scary, not official)
- Personality: confident, knowledgeable, but approachable and aspirational

## Current logo assessment (negative)

[[manik|Manik]]'s analysis: *"If I slapped your logo on a black wall..."*
- Generic feeling
- Feels archaic, not new-age
- Can get lost in crowd
- Forgettable and mundane
- Dull due to colors and execution
- Appears templatized (like logo maker output)
- **Verdict:** Would fail 10-year brand building test

This drove the eventual [[route-3-architecture-of-intelligence|Route 3 logo direction]].

## Packaging critique (emerging issues — fully resolved in [[2026-01-late-meeting-4-brand-identity|Meeting 4]])
- Too much written information
- Cluttered, confused appearance
- Doesn't match strategic "unfair advantage" language
- Lacks aspiration
- Feels too prescriptive/medicinal
- Does NOT communicate performance/growth mindset

## Competitive brands analyzed

Manik presented brands NOT in brain supplement space but showing relevant design directions. Detailed analysis happens in [[2026-01-late-meeting-4-brand-identity|Meeting 4]] but the references named here:

- **Simply Nootropics, Tonic, Neuro Gum, Onit** — explored
- **Whoop** — sophisticated health communication, data-driven, clean
- **AG1** — no-nonsense, clean, sporty, performance-driven, modern (aspirational reference)
- **Heights** — trendy, attention-grabbing, packaging shape differentiation
- **Über (unnamed brand)** — performance supplement with bold but not loud packaging

## Key visual insights from competitive analysis

Different brands communicate different briefs:
- **Heights brief:** *"I just want to look absolutely different"*
- **AG1 brief:** *"I want to seem clear, simple, modern, smart, stand for one thing"*
- **Whoop brief:** *"I know my stuff, have done work, tech-backed, designed for 1%, not a mass brand"*

## Logo / brand identity direction (initial)

Consensus emerging:
1. **Color scheme:** Monochrome language (not vibrant, not colorful)
   - Likely: Blackish-gray base + GREEN POP (leveraging poppy green of capsules)
2. **Visual language:** Performance-driven, new-tech aesthetic (similar to Nike's mindset + vigor + consistency)
3. **Packaging innovation:** Semi-transparent bottle to showcase green capsules (this becomes [[semi-transparent-bottle|the hero decision]] in Meeting 4)
4. **Overall aesthetic:** Less cluttered, more aspirational, approachable, sporty + performance + professional
