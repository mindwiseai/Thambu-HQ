# Meeting 1 — Brief Discussion

**Date:** 2026-01-06
**Attendees:** [[thambu]] (Lumen/Mindwise), [[aarti-samant]], [[tanya-leeds]] and [[sorted-agency|Sorted team]]
**Format:** Initial strategic briefing call

## Summary

The first formal meeting between [[lumen-marketing-company|Lumen]] and [[sorted-agency|Sorted Agency]] after engagement. Goal: establish foundational understanding of brand, product, and market positioning. [[thambu]] given space to narrate the **40-year origin story** including the [[adam-ritson]] catalyst arc. Output: [[four-cohorts|four target audiences]] crystallized, [[cdri-08]] USP clarity, market mapping.

## Key outputs

### The early brand idea (later replaced by [[your-unfair-advantage]])

> "Think for your brain today, so your brain can think for you tomorrow."

This was the workable platform line at this stage. It carried:
- Long-term cognitive wellness habit framing
- "I can do more with this product" mental performance angle (not hype)
- Target: high performers, students, professionals, aging populations

This was eventually superseded by [[your-unfair-advantage|"Your Unfair Advantage"]] in [[2026-01-23-meeting-3-brand-strategy-workshop|Meeting 3]].

### USP clarification crystallized (6 points)

1. **Original [[cdri-08]] extract** — not generic Bacopa (most competitors 20-30% bacosides; Mindwise [[55-percent-bacosides|55%+]])
2. **7+ gold-standard clinical trials** — memory, learning, information processing, attention, anxiety/stress
3. **Government backing** — [[csir-cdri|CSIR-CDRI]] origin (unique regulatory advantage)
4. **Patented extraction process** — seed-to-consumer quality control with batch consistency
5. **Long safety margin** — 40 years of pre-clinical toxicology + real-world use overseas
6. **Global trust record** — Millions of users globally for 20+ years (KeenMind, MemoPlus Gold, etc.)

### [[four-cohorts|Four target audiences defined]]

1. **Students & Exam-Goers (18-24)** — UPSC, JEE, board exams; personally/parent-motivated
2. **High-Pressure Professionals (25-45)** — Cognitive load, sustained performance, non-stimulant alternative
3. **Proactive Agers & Caregivers (40+)** — Memory decline, dementia prevention
4. **ADHD Individuals (18+)** — Focus and cognitive stability

### Persona sketches provided
- Parent (metro city) with 13-year-old struggling with focus; skeptical but science-open
- 24-year-old competitive exam preparer; YouTube productivity content consumer; non-addictive support
- 30-40 high performer; supplement-stack member; focus/retention enhancement
- 58-year-old noticing forgetfulness; skeptical of generic tonics; open to natural options with proof

### Business objectives for Year 1
1. **Sales Target:** 10,000 bottles/month (justifies ₹1.2-1.3 cr annual ad budget)
2. **Build retention base:** Repeat users via D2C + marketplaces (not one-time buyers)
3. **Establish brand communication:** Offline + online fully functional
4. **Foundation building:**
   - Awareness: clinically-backed daily supplement
   - Healthcare professional trust: build neurologist/psychiatrist/physician/psychologist prescriber base (this becomes [[brain-health-community-vision|the community vision]])
   - Differentiation: CDRI-08 distinct from generic bacopa

### [[placebo-wellness-enemy|Brand enemy]] identified
> "Placebo Wellness" — pretty labels, weak dosages, borrowed research

### Competitive landscape mapped
- **Tier 1 Direct (Bacopa-based):** Himalaya Brahmi, Organic India, unbranded generic bacopa
- **Tier 2 Indirect (Cognitive supplements):** Shilajit, Omega-3, Ginkgo, Magnesium, Ashwagandha multivitamins
- **Tier 3 Category:** Nootropics/smart drugs, energy drinks, coffee

### Marketing budget allocation (₹25 lakhs/year)
- Content: ₹10 lakhs
- Performance: ₹10 lakhs
- Influencer: ₹5 lakhs
