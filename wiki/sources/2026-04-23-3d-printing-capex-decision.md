# 3D-Printing Capex Decision + Accessory Catalogue Brief

## Summary

[[thambu]] is evaluating an in-house 3D-printing capability at [[racesims-company|RaceSims]] (Chennai) to produce a branded accessories catalogue and service B2B custom jobs. Capex recommendation: **Bambu Lab X1 Carbon Combo + accessories, ~₹1,55,000**. Catalogue brief locks a 10-SKU priority list across three two-week sprints, with material working set, quality protocol, and launch plan. ROI model shows ~3-month payback at steady state, ~6 months with realistic ramp, ~320% Year 1 ROI. Triggered by the [[online-instruments]] pivot to a half-cut-Thar simulator — first B2B custom candidate.

## Key facts

- **Capex total:** ₹1,55,000 (printer ₹1,30,000 + nozzles/plates/dryer/tools ₹25,000).
- **Printer selection:** [[bambu-lab-x1-carbon]] — enclosed chamber (ABS/ASA/PA-CF capable), 256×256×256 mm build, CoreXY speed, LIDAR auto-calibration, AMS 4-colour, Indian service via Dimensionex/3Ding/Wol3D.
- **Primary materials:** PETG (80% of work), ASA (branded/customer-visible), TPU (grips/pads), PA-CF (load-bearing). PLA explicitly NOT used for shipped parts (deforms at 55°C).
- **10 SKUs organized in three sprints:**
  - Sprint 1 (weeks 1–2): phone holder, cup holder, profile end caps, RaceSims logo badge.
  - Sprint 2 (weeks 3–4): LED strip channel, Philips Hue holder, Conspit 310 Apex side plate set, bass-shaker enclosure.
  - Sprint 3 (weeks 5–6): wind-sim fan housing, custom shift knob.
- **Steady-state monthly net profit:** ~₹53,334 on 70 units/mo mixed portfolio.
- **Year 1 projected net:** ~₹4,95,600 (with ramp). Payback 6 months realistic, 3 months steady-state.
- **Unquantified upside:** kit-bundle conversion lift, B2B custom margin, R&D iteration speed, cross-brand service revenue, inventory compression.
- **Race-engineer-voice hook:** every SKU carries "why a race engineer built this" copy; vibration-tested, heat-soaked, fit-verified before listing.

## Decisions

- **Printer:** [[bambu-lab-x1-carbon]] Combo + AMS, not Prusa MK4 / Creality K1 / resin alternatives. Rationale: enclosed chamber is non-negotiable for ASA/ABS/PA-CF in Indian conditions; CoreXY speed is production-viable; Indian service network exists.
- **Material discipline:** PETG and ASA are the workhorses. PLA is banned for shipped parts. PA-CF kept in reserve for load-bearing only (cost control).
- **Launch shape:** 8-week plan across 3 sprints, beta-ship to 5 existing customers free in weeks 1–2 for feedback before going live.
- **Sprint 1 SKUs:** Phone holder, cup holder, profile end caps, RaceSims logo badge — chosen for volume + workflow shakedown, not margin.
