# The Scary Drug Problem

The diagnosis that drove the entire [[positioning-shift|clinical → performance pivot]]. Surfaced by [[sorted-agency]] in [[2026-01-23-meeting-3-brand-strategy-workshop|Meeting 3]] when they distributed sample bottles of the existing [[domains/mindwise/_index|Mindwise]] packaging to their team.

## The reaction

Sorted's internal team, looking at the existing Mindwise bottle, asked:

> "Can I even have this? Should a doctor tell me?"

That single sentence crystallized the problem.

## What was causing it

1. **Over-information on the label.** The existing label had CDRI mentions, "Make in India" certifications, clinical claims, regulatory marks — all factually true, all visually overwhelming.
2. **Medicinal aesthetic.** White bottle, pharmacy-style typography, dense small type.
3. **Government / authority signals stacked too high.** Things meant to build trust read as "this is a controlled substance."
4. **Lack of any aspirational signal.** Nothing on the package said "this is something I want to have."

## Why it killed the brand
- **Killed casual trial.** People wouldn't pick it up off a shelf voluntarily.
- **Killed referrals.** No one shares "the supplement my doctor said I should consider."
- **Killed cross-age appeal.** See [[age-paradox]] — clinical aesthetic alienates 18-year-olds (they think it's for sick people) AND 80-year-olds (they think it's a drug).
- **Killed digital visibility.** Cluttered packaging photographs poorly. Performance ads need clean visuals.

## The reframe

Manik articulated the solution as: **stop being a "scary drug" and become a "formal drug."**

| Scary | Formal |
|---|---|
| Needs prescription | Professional, research-backed |
| Medical condition response | For optimization and performance |
| You take this when something is wrong | You take this to get an edge |
| Hospital pamphlet aesthetic | Whoop / AG1 / Nike aesthetic |

## What solved it

Two design decisions, both made in [[2026-01-late-meeting-4-brand-identity|Meeting 4]]:

1. **[[semi-transparent-bottle]]** — show the green capsules instead of hiding them. The product itself becomes the visual hero.
2. **Minimal copy on the bottle.** Push science to the website. The bottle's job is to intrigue, not to educate.

Plus the [[your-unfair-advantage|tagline]] which carries no clinical baggage.

## How to test future work

The reverse test: hand someone a Mindwise bottle (or look at an Instagram post / website page). Ask "would you pick this up / try this?" If their first instinct is to ask permission ("can I even take this?", "is this a drug?"), the work has failed.
